**[How Canada uses Claude: Findings from the Anthropic Economic Index](https://www.anthropic.com/research/how-canada-uses-claude)** — _Anthropic · Jul 14_

**Main takeaway:** Anthropic's first Canadian country brief from the Economic Index finds Canada at the forefront of Claude adoption: 2.6% of global Claude.ai traffic, 8th by total volume, and per-capita usage more than four times what its population would predict (AUI of 4.4) — second only to the US among the top ten countries by volume.

**Main methods:**
- **Adoption is regionally concentrated.** Ontario alone accounts for 43.9% of conversations, and the four largest provinces (with Quebec, BC, and Alberta) cover roughly 94% of national usage. Per capita, BC leads at 1.4x expected, Ontario at 1.1x, while every other province is below parity — Newfoundland and Labrador at 0.2x.
- **Industry mix explains the gaps, not income.** Unlike cross-country patterns, provincial income per capita doesn't explain adoption differences; provinces with large professional, scientific, and technical services sectors use Claude the most.
- **Usage mix is stable everywhere.** Work accounts for 34–40% of conversations in every province, coursework 13–18%, and personal use 44–51% — largely uncorrelated with adoption rates.
- **Translation tracks public administration.** New Brunswick, Nova Scotia, and Quebec have both the highest public-administration employment shares and the largest translation shares, likely reflecting Canada's official bilingualism in federal services. Document translation is also the most distinctive Canadian use case versus Anglosphere peers.
- **An academic, early-career tilt.** Academic coursework, coding assistance, and resume drafting are overrepresented in Canadian usage, while professional communication and everyday personal tasks are underrepresented. The analysis is based on a sample of Claude.ai conversations from February 2026.

**[加拿大人怎么用 Claude：Anthropic Economic Index 国别报告](https://www.anthropic.com/research/how-canada-uses-claude)** — _Anthropic · 7月14日_

**Main takeaway:** Anthropic Economic Index 的第一份加拿大国别报告显示，加拿大在 Claude 采用上跑在前列：占全球 Claude.ai 流量的 2.6%，总量排第 8，人均使用量是按人口预期的 4 倍多（AUI 4.4）。在用量最大的十个国家里，人均只输给美国。

**Main methods:**
- **省际差距很大。** 光安大略一省就占了 43.9% 的对话量，加上魁北克、BC 和阿尔伯塔，四大省合计约 94%。按人均算 BC 领先（预期的 1.4 倍），安大略 1.1 倍，其余省份全部低于平均线，纽芬兰与拉布拉多只有 0.2 倍。
- **解释差距的是产业结构，不是收入。** 跟跨国比较的规律不一样，省级人均收入解释不了省际差距；专业、科学和技术服务业占比高的省用得最多。
- **用途构成到哪都差不多。** 每个省的工作用途都在 34% 到 40% 之间，课程作业 13% 到 18%，个人用途 44% 到 51%，和采用率高低基本不相关。
- **翻译需求跟着公共部门走。** 新不伦瑞克、新斯科舍和魁北克的公共管理就业占比和翻译对话占比双双最高，大概率反映了加拿大联邦服务的官方双语政策。和其他英语国家比，文档翻译也是加拿大最有辨识度的用法。
- **偏学术、偏职业早期。** 课程作业、编程辅助和简历撰写在加拿大的用量都偏高，职场沟通和日常个人事务反而偏低。分析基于 2026 年 2 月的 Claude.ai 对话抽样。
