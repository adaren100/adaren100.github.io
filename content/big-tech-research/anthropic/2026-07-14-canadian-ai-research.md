**[Anthropic commits $10 million to Canadian AI research](https://www.anthropic.com/news/canadian-ai-research)** — _Anthropic · Jul 14_

**Main takeaway:** Anthropic is committing $10 million CAD to Canadian research institutions to fund beneficial and responsible applications of AI, with partnerships spanning Canada's three regional AI institutes (Amii, Mila, Vector) plus health and academic institutions. Alongside the funding, Anthropic published its first Canadian country brief from the Anthropic Economic Index.

**Main methods:**
- **The three AI institutes.** Amii (Edmonton) gets Claude credits for reinforcement learning and AI trust-and-safety work plus driving adoption across key sectors; Mila (Montréal) will make Claude available to its community for responsible AI, health, sustainability, multi-agent systems, and robotics, and will build AI assistants that help researchers discover and assess scientific breakthroughs; Vector (Toronto) will use credits for trust and safety plus health and science research.
- **Children's health and mental health.** CHEO will develop and evaluate AI-enabled approaches to improve care for children and families; CAMH's Krembil Centre for Neuroinformatics will build predictive models of mental-health treatment and run large-scale fairness evaluations of psychiatric AI systems, with credits also scaling multilingual AI-enabled mental health education.
- **More universities in the mix.** Université Laval's Institute for Intelligence and Data will study how LLMs behave, joined by the University of Toronto and University of Saskatchewan, with more partnerships promised in the coming months.
- **The historical framing.** Anthropic credits the University of Toronto and Université de Montréal with incubating neural-network research through the skepticism era, the University of Alberta with pioneering reinforcement learning, and Canadian institutions with demonstrating in the early 2010s that deep neural networks could succeed at scale on GPUs.
- **A companion data release.** The first Canadian country brief from the Anthropic Economic Index ships alongside, giving a snapshot of how Canadians use Claude.

**[Anthropic 给加拿大 AI 研究投 1000 万加元](https://www.anthropic.com/news/canadian-ai-research)** — _Anthropic · 7月14日_

**Main takeaway:** Anthropic 宣布向加拿大研究机构投入 1000 万加元，资助 AI 的有益和负责任应用，合作方包括加拿大三大区域 AI 研究院（Amii、Mila、Vector），还拉上了几家医疗和高校机构。随投资一起发布的还有 Anthropic Economic Index 的第一份加拿大国别报告。

**Main methods:**
- **三大 AI 研究院怎么用。** 埃德蒙顿的 Amii 拿 Claude credits 做 reinforcement learning 和 AI trust and safety，顺带推动关键行业的 AI 采用；蒙特利尔的 Mila 会把 Claude 开放给社区，用在负责任 AI、健康、可持续、multi-agent 系统和机器人方向，还要用 Claude 做帮研究者发现和评估科学突破的 AI 助手；多伦多的 Vector 主攻 trust and safety 加健康与科学研究。
- **儿童健康和心理健康。** CHEO 用来开发和评估改善儿童与家庭医疗体验的 AI 方案；CAMH 的 Krembil 神经信息学中心要做心理健康治疗的预测模型，并对精神科 AI 系统跑大规模公平性 eval，credits 还会用来扩大多语言 AI 心理健康教育的覆盖。
- **更多高校也在名单里。** Université Laval 的智能与数据研究所研究 LLM 的行为规律，多伦多大学和萨斯喀彻温大学也在列，后面几个月还会有新的合作方加入。
- **历史叙事。** Anthropic 特意点了加拿大的老本：神经网络研究最不被看好的年代，多伦多大学和蒙特利尔大学是少数几家坚持孵化这条路线的机构，阿尔伯塔大学开创了 reinforcement learning，2010 年代初也是加拿大的研究机构率先证明了深度神经网络配上 GPU 能大规模成功。
- **配套数据发布。** Anthropic Economic Index 的首份加拿大国别报告同步上线，速览加拿大人都在拿 Claude 干什么。
