**[Improving health intelligence in ChatGPT](https://openai.com/index/improving-health-intelligence-in-chatgpt)** — _OpenAI · Jun 18_

**Main takeaway:** GPT-5.5 Instant — now available to all free ChatGPT users — reaches health-evaluation performance comparable to OpenAI's frontier Thinking models, and physician reviewers rated its responses higher than both older models and physician-written responses across 3,500 reviewed cases.

**Main methods:**
- **Scale of the use case.** Over 230 million people per week use ChatGPT for health questions — making sense of lab results, preparing for appointments, navigating insurance, building habits — which motivates pushing better health behavior into the free-tier Instant model.
- **HealthBench-driven evaluation.** Progress measured on HealthBench and HealthBench Professional, which use realistic health conversations and physician-written rubrics scoring accuracy, safety, communication, context awareness, completeness, and appropriate escalation.
- **5.5 Instant ≈ Thinking on aggregate health evals.** GPT-5.5 Instant reaches performance similar to the latest Thinking models on the aggregate, a substantial jump from GPT-5.3 Instant released only two months earlier.
- **Head-to-head against physicians.** A separate panel of physicians compared model responses to physician-written responses (written with internet access but no AI) across 3,500 conversations; GPT-5.5 Instant came out higher on accuracy, communication, completeness, instruction following, and decision helpfulness.
- **Fewer failure modes.** Physicians rated 5.5 Instant as having fewer failure modes than prior models, particularly around recognizing when urgent care may be needed, asking for relevant context, and explaining uncertainty without overstating confidence.
- **Distribution choice matters.** OpenAI emphasizes that pushing these gains into the free Instant tier (not just Thinking) is what makes the improvement broadly accessible.

**[ChatGPT 在健康咨询上又上了一个档](https://openai.com/index/improving-health-intelligence-in-chatgpt)** — _OpenAI · 6月18日_

**Main takeaway:** GPT-5.5 Instant 在健康类 evaluation 上的整体表现已经跟 OpenAI 的 frontier Thinking model 持平，而且在 3,500 个由 physician 评审的对话里，被打分高于此前的模型，也高于 physician 自己写的回答。关键是这个版本对所有免费用户都开放。

**Main methods:**
- **使用规模就摆在那。** 每周有超过 2.3 亿人用 ChatGPT 问健康相关的问题——看懂化验单、准备问诊、搞清楚保险、养成习惯，所以把更好的医疗回答能力推进免费版的 Instant model 很值。
- **用 HealthBench 来量进度。** evaluation 跑的是 HealthBench 和 HealthBench Professional，用真实的就诊对话加 physician 写的 rubric 给 accuracy、safety、communication、context awareness、completeness、appropriate escalation 打分。
- **5.5 Instant 整体追上了 Thinking。** 在健康 eval 的聚合分数上，5.5 Instant 跟最新的 Thinking model 基本持平，相比两个月前发的 5.3 Instant 是个明显跳跃。
- **跟 physician 直接 PK。** 另一组 physician 把模型回答和 physician 自己写的回答（可以查互联网，但不能用 AI）做对比，3,500 个对话里 5.5 Instant 在准确性、表达清楚程度、完整性、follow instruction、对决策的帮助上都更胜一筹。
- **failure mode 明显少了。** physician 觉得 5.5 Instant 比前代少犯错的地方，主要在能不能识别需要紧急就医、会不会主动追问背景信息、以及在不确定的时候能不能老实说"不太确定"。
- **怎么发也很关键。** OpenAI 特别强调把这些提升推进了免费版的 Instant，而不是只在 Thinking 里，这样真正能用到的人才多。
