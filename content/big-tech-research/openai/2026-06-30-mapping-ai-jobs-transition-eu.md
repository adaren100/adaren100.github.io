**[Mapping Europe's AI Workforce Opportunity](https://openai.com/index/mapping-ai-jobs-transition-eu)** — _OpenAI · Jun 29_

**Main takeaway:** OpenAI Economic Research extended its AI Jobs Transition Framework to the EU using the ESCO taxonomy and Eurostat data, finding that roughly 14% of EU employment sits in occupations with higher near-term automation potential and 27% in occupations likely to reorganize workflows — with notable variation across member states.

**Main methods:**
- **ESCO taxonomy and Eurostat data.** The analysis maps official EU occupational categories (European Skills, Competences, Qualifications and Occupations) to AI capability estimates alongside Eurostat employment figures to estimate transition exposure by country.
- **Four transition archetypes.** Occupations are classified as: may grow with AI (~12% of employment), higher near-term automation potential (~14%), likely to reorganize (~27%), and less immediate change (~47%). These are planning categories, not employment forecasts.
- **Country-level divergence.** Luxembourg, Sweden, and the Netherlands have larger shares of occupations that may grow with AI; Germany, Greece, and Italy have larger shares in higher-automation-potential roles — reflecting differences in occupational structure, not policy.
- **Earlier-signals framing.** The framework is designed to flag transition pressure in occupation-level statistics before changes appear in headline labor-market data, giving policymakers and educators lead time to act.
- **EU vs. US comparison.** The EU has a smaller share of employment in high-automation-potential occupations than the US — attributed to differences in institutional context and licensing systems that slow workforce change.

**[欧洲 AI 劳动力机遇全景图](https://openai.com/index/mapping-ai-jobs-transition-eu)** — _OpenAI · 6月29日_

**Main takeaway:** OpenAI 经济研究团队把他们的 AI Jobs Transition Framework 延伸到了欧盟，用 ESCO 职业分类体系加上 Eurostat 就业数据做分析，发现大概 14% 的欧盟就业岗位面临较高的近期自动化风险，另有 27% 的工作可能会经历流程重组——但各成员国的情况差异相当大。

**Main methods:**
- **ESCO 分类 + Eurostat 数据。** 把欧盟官方职业技能体系（ESCO）和 AI 能力估算对上，再结合 Eurostat 就业数据，逐职业、逐国家估算转型暴露度。
- **四种转型类型。** 把职业分成四类：随 AI 可能增长（约 12%）、近期自动化潜力较高（约 14%）、工作流程可能重组（约 27%）、短期变化较小（约 47%）。这是规划地图，不是就业预测。
- **各国差异明显。** 卢森堡、瑞典、荷兰的"AI 助推增长"职业比例更高；德国、希腊、意大利的高自动化风险职业比例更大——背后是各国职业结构的差异，不是政策好坏的问题。
- **领先信号思路。** 这套框架的用意是在宏观劳动市场数据还没动之前，就从职业层面的统计里发现转型压力，给政策制定者和教育者留出反应时间。
- **欧盟 vs 美国。** 欧盟高自动化风险职业的就业占比低于美国，主要原因是欧洲有更多执照制度和本地化的交付场景，职业转型本来就更慢。
