### Box CEO Aaron Levie

Box CEO Aaron Levie made the case that agents are moving past coding into the rest of knowledge work — but to get there, we need ways of bridging the model's general capabilities with industry-specific workflows. The models stay general purpose; what gets specialized is how agents are aligned to the unique workflows, domain context, and data sources of legal, financial services, insurance, healthcare, and life sciences. His concrete example: Claude just shipped an updated set of plugins and skills for the legal industry, including Box — meaning you can now take any enterprise contract or document and work with it headlessly via Claude inside a legal workflow. He sees this as a preview of both industry-specific AI adoption and what headless software interaction looks like going forward. (https://x.com/levie/status/2054277953794847152)

Box CEO Aaron Levie 给了一个判断：agent 正在从写代码外溢到其他知识工作，但要真正落地，得想办法把模型的通用能力和具体行业的工作流接起来。模型本身保持通用，特化的是 agent 怎么去对齐 legal、金融服务、保险、医疗、生命科学这些行业各自独有的工作流、领域 context 和数据源。他举的例子很具体：Claude 刚刚上了一批面向法律行业的 plugin 和 skill，里面就包含 Box——意味着你现在可以把任何企业合同或文档放进去，在 Claude 里以 headless 的方式跑一套法律工作流。他觉得这只是开头，行业特化的 AI 落地和"headless 软件交互"未来的样子，差不多就是这个路径。(https://x.com/levie/status/2054277953794847152)

### Y Combinator CEO Garry Tan

It's that AI lets you verify at a level that was previously too expensive to sustain." His point: the 90% testing threshold is magical, but it used to cost too much human willpower to reach — now it's effectively free. (https://x.com/garrytan/status/2054065076852625582)

Y Combinator CEO Garry Tan 今天给"AI 真正的杠杆点在哪"做了个很犀利的 reframe："关键不是 AI 让你写代码更快——这个事大家早就发现了。关键是 AI 让你能把 verify 这件事做到一个以前根本撑不住的密度。"他的意思是：90% 测试覆盖率这个阈值其实一直很 magical，只是过去要走到那里太费人的意志力——现在这部分基本上免费了。(https://x.com/garrytan/status/2054065076852625582)

### Google DeepMind

Google DeepMind shared a thread on what an AI-enabled mouse pointer could unlock — and it's a sharper reframe than it sounds. For decades, your mouse only tracked where you were pointing; AI lets it understand what you're pointing at. The example: a photo of a scribbled note becomes an interactive to-do list, or a paused video frame becomes a restaurant booking link. The bigger thesis is that in the real world we don't speak in long paragraphs — we point and say "fix this" or "move that" — and combining gestures with speech lets you complete tasks in that same natural shorthand. They're treating this as a guide for the next generation of interfaces, with experiments live in Google AI Studio. (https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2054246128221143399, https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2054246130511225053, https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2054246132222419226)

Google DeepMind 发了一组 thread，讲一个"AI 加持的鼠标指针"能解锁什么——这个 reframe 比听起来要狠。过去几十年，鼠标只能告诉系统你"指向哪里"；AI 让它开始理解你"指的是什么东西"。他们举的例子很直观：随手拍一张写满字的便签照片，可以直接变成一个可交互的 to-do list；视频暂停的某一帧，可以直接变成一个餐厅订位链接。更大的论点是：现实世界里人本来就不靠长段话沟通——我们是一边指一边说"把这个改一下"、"把那个挪一下"——把手势和语音组合起来，就能用这种最自然的简写方式把任务交给系统去做。他们把这个看作下一代界面的指引方向，相关实验已经放进 Google AI Studio 了。(https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2054246128221143399, https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2054246130511225053, https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2054246132222419226)
