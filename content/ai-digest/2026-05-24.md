### Box CEO Aaron Levie

Box CEO Aaron Levie pushed back on the "AI is eliminating jobs" framing with a long take: we keep confusing task completion with eliminating the whole job. Even when AI automates one or many tasks inside a role, the definition of the job almost inevitably just expands — doing more of those tasks, doing them at higher quality, or moving on to whatever hasn't been automated yet. That expansion is exactly what makes the job newly valuable, and often newly accessible to audiences who couldn't afford it before. The small business that wants to take on bigger software projects, or the SMB that couldn't afford a marketing agency, can now hire someone with agents. His one-liner: don't fall into the trap of confusing tasks with jobs ([link](https://x.com/levie/status/2058223867815227756)).

Box CEO Aaron Levie 反驳了"AI 会消灭工作"这种叙事，写了一段挺长的看法：我们一直在混淆"AI 完成了某个任务"和"AI 取代了整个 job"。哪怕 AI 把一个岗位里的某项或多项任务自动化掉了，这个岗位的定义几乎一定会顺势扩张——做更多同类任务、把任务做得更精细，或者去做那些还没被自动化的部分。正是这种扩张让这个岗位拥有了新的价值，并且常常面向一群之前根本请不起的客户。原来想做大型软件项目却请不起人的小公司，现在能找人配合 agent 做了；原来请不起 marketing agency 的小公司，现在也能请到一个能干一整队人活的 marketer 了。他的一句话总结：别掉进把 task 和 job 混为一谈的陷阱 ([链接](https://x.com/levie/status/2058223867815227756))。

### OpenClaw founder Peter Steinberger

OpenClaw founder Peter Steinberger shared two useful Codex workflow notes from a real refactor. He's currently rewriting an older subagents module that touches a lot of code, and his autoreview loop has been running for 5+ hours and is still fixing tons of issues — a concrete data point for what "let it cook" looks like on a big refactor ([link](https://x.com/steipete/status/2058307930613518698)). His tip from that experience: tell Codex to maintain a scratch-log as it works on bigger refactors — decisions it had to make, tradeoffs, review fixes — so you can later read through the agent's reasoning and figure out what you forgot to specify ([link](https://x.com/steipete/status/2058308112134635528)).

OpenClaw 创始人 Peter Steinberger 从一次真实的重构里分享了两条 Codex 使用心得。他正在重写一块涉及面很广的老 subagents 模块，autoreview 已经连跑了 5 个多小时，仍然在不停修问题——给"放手让它跑"在大型 refactor 上到底是什么样，提供了一个具体数据点 ([链接](https://x.com/steipete/status/2058307930613518698))。从这次经验里他总结的小技巧是：让 Codex 在做大型 refactor 时维护一份 scratch-log，把它做过的决策、权衡、review 修复都记下来，事后你可以翻回去看 agent 是怎么想的，也能发现自己当初哪些地方没说清楚 ([链接](https://x.com/steipete/status/2058308112134635528))。

### South Park Commons GP Aditya Agarwal

South Park Commons GP Aditya Agarwal (ex Dropbox CTO) dropped a short reflection on how fast the ground has shifted: just six months ago, in November 2025, we were mostly chatting with LLMs and being thrilled about AI. By May 2026, these same LLMs have produced more code than humans have written across all of history ([link](https://x.com/adityaag/status/2058233900464238801)).

South Park Commons 的 GP Aditya Agarwal（前 Dropbox CTO）感慨了一下脚下变化有多快：仅仅 6 个月前、也就是 2025 年 11 月，我们大多数时候还只是在跟 LLM 聊天，并且觉得这就已经很激动人心了。到了 2026 年 5 月，这些 LLM 写出的代码量已经超过了人类有史以来写过的所有代码 ([链接](https://x.com/adityaag/status/2058233900464238801))。
