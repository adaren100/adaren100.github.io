### Box CEO Aaron Levie

Box CEO Aaron Levie laid out why the Forward Deployed Engineer (FDE) job category will stick around as long as AI keeps moving fast. His argument: cloud adoption mostly affected a concentrated set of users (devs and IT) and didn't require employees to change their workflows. Agents are the opposite — implementing them is both highly technical AND directly reshapes the workflows people participate in, so you get heavy technical work plus heavy change management. On top of that, every new model release either unlocks something that wasn't possible before or makes existing scaffolding redundant, so best practices never have time to settle. Net result: most enterprises will keep needing a vendor or partner who has seen the implementation hundreds or thousands of times, even with strong internal support. He's calling FDE a great path for technical talent, especially early career ([link](https://x.com/levie/status/2057315272156135501)).

Box CEO Aaron Levie 解释了为什么 Forward Deployed Engineer（FDE）这个岗位在 AI 还在快速变化的时期会一直存在。他的论点是：当年云的普及主要冲击的是一群很集中的用户（开发者、IT），员工的 workflow 基本不用动；但 agent 完全相反——上线既高度技术化、又会直接改变员工日常工作的 workflow，所以会同时叠加大量技术工作和繁重的变革管理。再加上每次模型升级要么解锁一类全新能力、要么让你之前搭的某层脚手架变得多余，best practice 根本来不及沉淀。结果就是：哪怕客户内部有很强的团队，绝大多数企业还是需要一个"已经做过几百上千次类似项目"的厂商或合作方来帮忙落地。他把 FDE 看作技术人才（尤其 early career）的一条很好的路 ([链接](https://x.com/levie/status/2057315272156135501))。

### South Park Commons GP Aditya Agarwal

South Park Commons GP Aditya Agarwal (ex Dropbox CTO) wrote four blunt takes on early-stage hiring: (1) if an engineer is still trying to choose between a pre Series-B startup and a BigCo/BigLab, stop talking to them immediately — they're not ready for a startup; (2) if they won't take a 70% cash paycut vs BigCo, stop talking — they'll just end up unhappy and stressed; (3) you learn a lot about a candidate during negotiation and closing, so don't be afraid to walk away if new information surfaces; (4) startups have zero work-life balance, and if someone isn't willing to put in the hours they're not in the right headspace to grind ([link](https://x.com/adityaag/status/2057502381038846306)).

South Park Commons 的 GP Aditya Agarwal（前 Dropbox CTO）写了四条挺直白的早期招聘观点：(1) 如果一个 engineer 还在 pre Series-B 的创业公司和 BigCo / BigLab 之间犹豫，立刻停止聊——这个人还没准备好做 startup；(2) 如果对方不愿意接受相对 BigCo 砍掉 70% 现金的 offer，立刻停止聊——进来之后大概率不开心、压力大；(3) 谈 offer / 收尾这段过程能让你学到很多关于候选人的事，出现新信息时不要害怕直接走人；(4) 创业完全没有 work-life balance，不愿意 grind 的人就不在正确的状态里 ([链接](https://x.com/adityaag/status/2057502381038846306))。
