### Latent Space and AI Engineer founder Swyx

Swyx doubled down on his call that "coding agents breaking containment" is the breakout theme of 2026 — meaning this is the year all knowledge workers, not just coders, get AGI-pilled. In a short closing talk for AIE EU he showed how his own org runs as a Tiny Team using agents (OpenClaw personally, Devin and townai as a team) to serve ~1m unique developers a month for everything from CMS work to renting lobster inflatables, and his message to viewers is blunt: most people are not trying hard enough to push agents into their daily knowledge work. ([tweet](https://x.com/swyx/status/2050068468498842058))

Latent Space 与 AI Engineer 创始人 Swyx 进一步强调，他认为 2026 年最具突破性的主题就是"coding agents breaking containment"——也就是说，今年不只是程序员，所有知识工作者都将被 AGI 击中。他在 AIE EU 的 closing note 上做了一段短演讲，展示了自己的团队如何作为一个 Tiny Team，通过 agent（个人用 OpenClaw，团队用 Devin 和 townai）每月服务约 100 万开发者，从 CMS 到租用龙虾充气玩具的各种事都能搞定。他给观众的话很直接：大多数人在把 agent 推入日常知识工作上还不够用力。([推文](https://x.com/swyx/status/2050068468498842058))

### Box CEO Aaron Levie

Aaron Levie posted a long, widely-shared piece on what software business models look like once agents — not people — are the dominant users. His framework: (1) human seats stay, but every seat must come bundled with a healthy quota of API usage so that the user can interact with the data via ChatGPT, Codex, Claude, Gemini, Cursor, Copilot, Perplexity and friends — "if you don't do this, you're DOA"; (2) agents may have their own seat-like SKU when they hold persistent state, workspaces and distinct permissions, OpenClaw-style; (3) above the seat allotment the dominant model will be consumption-based pricing on APIs, sometimes packaged as outcome-shaped APIs that represent a whole unit of agent work rather than a granular call. He frames the overall growth as effectively unbounded because agents will operate on data far more than humans ever did. ([tweet](https://x.com/levie/status/2050051426446152159))

Box CEO Aaron Levie 发了一篇被广泛转发的长贴，谈在 agent 而非人成为软件主要用户后，软件商业模式会怎么变。他的框架是：(1) 面向人的 seat 不会消失，但每一个 seat 都必须捆绑一定额度的 API 调用，让用户可以通过 ChatGPT、Codex、Claude、Gemini、Cursor、Copilot、Perplexity 等去使用这些数据——"不这么做的公司直接就是 DOA"；(2) 当 agent 拥有自己的持久状态、工作空间和与用户不同的权限时，它们也可能拥有类似 seat 的 SKU，类似 OpenClaw 那种形态；(3) 超出 seat 配额或纯粹由 agent 自主完成的工作，主导定价模式将是按用量计费的 consumption model，部分场景里可能要做成"按结果计费"的新型 API，把 agent 一次完整的工作打包，而不是按一次次调用计费。他认为整体增长几乎没有上限，因为 agent 在系统数据上的操作量将远远超过人类。([推文](https://x.com/levie/status/2050051426446152159))

Levie also pushed back on Wall Street's surprise at Atlassian's results with a simple heuristic: in a world with 100x more agents than people, look at the parts of software whose volume goes up because agents are doing more underlying work — code generation, lead generation, contract review, invoice processing, design, payments — and the systems of record sitting underneath that work (storage, security, compliance, workflows) only go up over time. ([tweet](https://x.com/levie/status/2050295657836277764))

Levie 还回应了华尔街对 Atlassian 财报的惊讶，他给出的判断方式很简单：在一个 agent 数量是人 100 倍的世界里，去看哪些软件的使用量会因为 agent 干更多活而被推高——code 生成、线索生成、合同审阅、发票处理、设计、支付——以及这些工作背后承接的 system of record（存储、安全、合规、workflow），这些底层系统只会随着时间不断变大。([推文](https://x.com/levie/status/2050295657836277764))

A third post from Levie reports that most enterprises he talks to outside Silicon Valley don't see AI primarily as cost-cutting — they see it as a way to break through bottlenecks they've always had, ship more product, onboard customers faster, and bring intelligence and automation to areas that the tech industry's geographic concentration never reached. Where there is cost-cutting, he argues, it usually funds growth elsewhere or evaporates as competitors use AI to win on customer experience. ([tweet](https://x.com/levie/status/2050240083325030404))

Levie 的第三条贴子谈到，硅谷之外大多数企业在他眼里并不主要把 AI 当作降本工具——它们更多把 AI 看成突破长期瓶颈、加速出货、更快完成客户上手、把"智能化"和自动化带到此前因技术行业地理集中而被忽视的领域的方式。即便有成本节约，他认为通常也是用来支援其他领域的增长，否则也会被同样使用 AI 的竞争对手以更好的客户体验抹掉。([推文](https://x.com/levie/status/2050240083325030404))

### Y Combinator President Garry Tan

Garry Tan made an unusually concrete economic argument against Seattle's data center moratorium: a one-gigawatt data center complex generates roughly 5,322 permanent jobs, $157M/year in state taxes, $248M/year in local taxes, and $2.67B in combined investment during construction — all of which Seattle has banned. He also slammed proposed asset-seizure tax measures in California as a fast way to drive billionaires (and the tax base they fund) out of the state. ([tweet 1](https://x.com/garrytan/status/2050348945545810154), [tweet 2](https://x.com/garrytan/status/2050365216421241152))

YC 总裁 Garry Tan 罕见地用一组很具体的经济数字反驳 Seattle 对数据中心的禁令：一个 1GW 的数据中心综合体能创造约 5,322 个长期岗位、每年 1.57 亿美元州税、2.48 亿美元地方税，建设期合计 26.7 亿美元投资——而 Seattle 已经把这一切禁了。他也批评加州正在讨论的"资产没收式"税收提案，认为这是把亿万富翁（以及他们撑起的整个税基）赶出加州的最快方式。([推文 1](https://x.com/garrytan/status/2050348945545810154), [推文 2](https://x.com/garrytan/status/2050365216421241152))

In a more personal note, Tan imported 17 years of Foursquare check-ins (5000+) into OpenClaw and now uses it to generate travel guides of his favorite places, suggesting OpenClaw / Hermes might finally make those long-abandoned "Download Your Data" links from dead social services genuinely useful. ([tweet](https://x.com/garrytan/status/2050338264260006093))

在更个人的一面，Tan 把自己 17 年里超过 5000 条的 Foursquare 打卡数据导入到 OpenClaw 里，用它生成自己心爱地点的旅行指南，并感慨 OpenClaw / Hermes 也许能让那些早已死掉的社交产品里"Download Your Data"按钮第一次真正变得有用起来。([推文](https://x.com/garrytan/status/2050338264260006093))

### FPV Ventures partner Nikunj Kothari

Nikunj Kothari shipped his first paid side project: Family Home Finder, a one-chat / 11-question tool that produces a print-ready report covering schools, budget, neighborhood, walkability and comps for families house-hunting in a new city. It's built on Railway, Conductor and Claude with Stripe for payments, priced roughly at cost ($8–9 per report), and he's offering active house-hunters a half-price code in exchange for feedback. ### Every CEO Dan Shipper

Dan Shipper offered a sharp framing of where models stand today: "models know more than any individual human, but any individual human learns faster than models do" — a one-line argument for why "smarter than the smartest human" doesn't yet erase the human edge in fast-moving domains. ([tweet](https://x.com/danshipper/status/2050304359024759242))

Every CEO Dan Shipper 给出了一句对模型现状的精炼判断："模型比任何一个个体人类知道得多，但任何一个个体人类的学习速度都比模型快"——一句话解释了为什么"比最聪明的人还聪明"目前还不能完全抹掉人类在快速变化的领域里的优势。([推文](https://x.com/danshipper/status/2050304359024759242))
