### AI Engineer organizer Swyx

AI Engineer organizer Swyx put out a 4-day-left reminder for talk submissions at the upcoming World's Fair, with one important addition: this is the first year they'll also run preprint poster sessions for research papers, so researchers can submit through the same flow even though a dedicated process is still in the works ([link](https://x.com/swyx/status/2059372579790741793)). On the way there, he noted this June marks the 3-year anniversary of his "Rise of the AI Engineer" post, with AIE EU, MIA, and SG together hitting 8M views and ~1.5M unique AI engineers year-to-date — numbers he expects the World's Fair to dwarf ([link](https://x.com/swyx/status/2059372330032484741)). He also flagged a counter-narrative worth tracking: everyone talks about the China-to-US catchup, not enough people talk about the US-to-China catchup, calling out work from o_lacombe and robert_mchardy ([link](https://x.com/swyx/status/2059352284669370837)).

AI Engineer 大会的组织者 Swyx 提醒大家，World's Fair 的 talk 投稿只剩 4 天了，并加了一条重要更新：这是他们第一次为研究论文设 preprint poster session，研究者可以走同一个投稿入口提交（虽然真正独立的流程还在搭）([链接](https://x.com/swyx/status/2059372579790741793))。顺带一提，今年 6 月正好是他那篇"Rise of the AI Engineer"博客的 3 周年——AIE EU、MIA、SG 加起来到目前已经累计 8M 次观看、约 1.5M 独立 AI engineer，他预计 World's Fair 会把这些数字再拉一个量级 ([链接](https://x.com/swyx/status/2059372330032484741))。他还甩出了一个值得关注的反向叙事：大家都在聊 China→US 的追赶，关注 US→China 这条线的人远远不够，特别点名了 o_lacombe 和 robert_mchardy 的工作 ([链接](https://x.com/swyx/status/2059352284669370837))。

### Roblox Product lead Peter Yang

Roblox Product lead Peter Yang ran his own A/B test between Codex and Claude and gave a clean split: Codex is very good — he was especially impressed by how it uses the browser to test its own work — but for any design-related or frontend tasks, Claude still wins ([link](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2059099566377693305)). He also dropped a memorable framing on the current pricing moment: think of tokenmaxxing as eating crab legs at the buffet — the all-you-can-eat AI plans aren't going to last forever, so eat the crab legs while you still can ([link](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2059070818798465330)).

Roblox 的产品负责人 Peter Yang 自己跑了一次 Codex vs Claude 的 A/B 测试，结论分得很干净：Codex 确实强——特别是它会用浏览器去自我验证产出，这一点很惊艳——但凡是涉及设计或前端的任务，Claude 还是赢家 ([链接](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2059099566377693305))。他还给当下的定价时刻贴了一个很形象的比喻：把 tokenmaxx 想成自助餐里的蟹脚——这种"AI 任你吃"的套餐不可能永远存在，所以趁还能拿就多拿 ([链接](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2059070818798465330))。

### Claude Code engineer Thariq

Claude Code engineer Thariq shared his go-to trick for using Claude Code on non-technical work: throw a bunch of files into a folder and tell it that it can write scripts and make HTML. From there, the pattern is consistent — image or video editing becomes "write scripts"; finances, tax work, and medical advice become "drop in PDFs, write scripts, output HTML"; paperwork becomes scripts; reports and plans become HTML documents ([link](https://x.com/trq212/status/2059363113963540788), [link](https://x.com/trq212/status/2059363115146395965)). The other half of the recipe is connecting data — Gmail, Google Calendar, and similar connectors matter, but he thinks people underestimate just how much useful context they already have sitting in local files ([link](https://x.com/trq212/status/2059363116316598739)).

Claude Code 工程师 Thariq 分享了他用 Claude Code 处理非技术工作的核心技巧：把一堆文件丢进一个文件夹，然后告诉它可以写脚本、生成 HTML。从这里开始模式就很统一了——图片或视频编辑变成"写脚本"；财务、报税、医疗咨询变成"塞 PDF + 写脚本 + 输出 HTML"；填表变脚本；写报告和做计划变 HTML 文档 ([链接](https://x.com/trq212/status/2059363113963540788)、[链接](https://x.com/trq212/status/2059363115146395965))。另一半诀窍是让它能拿到数据——Gmail、Google Calendar 这种 connector 当然重要，但他认为大家严重低估了自己本地文件里已经有多少可用的 context ([链接](https://x.com/trq212/status/2059363116316598739))。

### FirstMark VC Matt Turck

FirstMark VC Matt Turck floated what he calls the biggest mindf*ck scenario in AI: things don't change that much. Both doomers and accelerationists turn out to be wrong; we're all just more productive, agents deliver automation in the enterprise, some important scientific discoveries get made — all great. But that's it ([link](https://x.com/mattturck/status/2059411493196529751)).

FirstMark 的 VC Matt Turck 抛了一个他认为 AI 领域最让人脑震荡的情景：什么都没怎么变。doomer 和 accelerationist 最后都被打脸；大家只是变得更高效一些，agent 在企业里完成了一些自动化，一些重要的科学发现也确实出来了——挺好的。但也就这样了 ([链接](https://x.com/mattturck/status/2059411493196529751))。

### FPV Ventures partner Nikunj Kothari

FPV Ventures partner Nikunj Kothari posted a sharp thesis: every venture-backed application company needs to inherently be a data company and/or a fintech company. Ideally both. If you're not, find a way to quickly get there ([link](https://x.com/nikunj/status/2059424310079697188)).

FPV Ventures 的合伙人 Nikunj Kothari 抛出了一个很犀利的判断：所有 VC 投的应用层公司，本质上都需要成为一家 data 公司，或者一家 fintech 公司——最好两者都是。如果你不是，赶紧想办法变成 ([链接](https://x.com/nikunj/status/2059424310079697188))。

### OpenClaw founder Peter Steinberger

OpenClaw founder Peter Steinberger kept pulling at his dependency thread. The deps around opus were all old or terrible, so he vibed his own and replaced octoscript and opus-native — modern WASM on Node/V8 is now roughly equivalent to native in performance, which unlocks features like the claw automatically taking meeting notes and letting you talk to it in meetings ([link](https://x.com/steipete/status/2059422568352714981)). He also factored their image-handling logic out into a standalone library, Rastermill — portable image processing for Node agents, Wasm+Rust under the hood, designed in part to keep small malicious images from blowing up your process ([link](https://x.com/steipete/status/2059423344961671290)). On a more operational note, he asked the room what people are using for SSO/SCIM/endpoint security in 2026 as he hires for the OpenClaw Foundation ([link](https://x.com/steipete/status/2059421603268608302)).

OpenClaw 创始人 Peter Steinberger 继续在依赖这条线上做减法。opus 周围那些依赖要么老要么烂，他干脆自己 vibe 了一套，替掉了 octoscript 和 opus-native——现代 WASM 在 Node/V8 上的性能已经基本等同 native，这一波改完之后，claw 就能自动做会议记录，也能在会议里跟它对话 ([链接](https://x.com/steipete/status/2059422568352714981))。他还把他们的图像处理逻辑抽成了一个独立库 Rastermill——一个面向 Node agent 的便携式图像处理库，底下跑 Wasm+Rust，部分动机是为了不让那些被刻意构造的小图片把进程炸掉 ([链接](https://x.com/steipete/status/2059423344961671290))。另外他在为 OpenClaw Foundation 招人，顺手问了大家：2026 年这个时间点，SSO/SCIM/endpoint security 都在用什么 ([链接](https://x.com/steipete/status/2059421603268608302))。

### Google DeepMind

Google DeepMind highlighted their new Gemini for Science suite, framed as tools to help researchers unlock their next breakthrough ([link](https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2059302777818325236)). They also shipped two updates on content provenance. SynthID verification in Gemini has now been used 50+ million times to check whether media was AI-generated — and they're scaling it further by expanding that "Is this made with AI?" check directly into Search and Google Chrome ([link](https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2059235184130535436)). For videos filmed on Pixel, they're adding a transparency trail that records how media was created and modified, with or without AI, starting from the moment you hit record ([link](https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2059235187003642154)).

Google DeepMind 重点介绍了他们的新工具集 Gemini for Science，定位是帮研究者跑出下一个突破 ([链接](https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2059302777818325236))。同一天他们在内容溯源上也推了两个更新。Gemini 里的 SynthID verification 现在累计被调用了 5000 万次以上来判断一段内容是不是 AI 生成的——他们准备把这个"这是不是 AI 做的？"的能力直接拓展到 Search 和 Google Chrome ([链接](https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2059235184130535436))。对于 Pixel 上拍的视频，他们也加了一条 transparency trail：从你按下录制键的那一刻起，就开始记录这段内容是怎么被创建、被修改的，无论中间有没有用 AI ([链接](https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2059235187003642154))。

### Anthropic

Anthropic published a new Engineering Blog piece making the case that the access and permissions we grant agents should evolve with their capabilities. In their own products, they set those parameters through sandboxing, which limits the scope of any potentially destructive actions — a practical framing for anyone building tool-using agents right now ([link](https://x.com/AnthropicAI/status/2059351260243919269)).

Anthropic 在他们的 Engineering Blog 上发了一篇新文章，核心观点是：我们授予 agent 的权限和访问范围应当随它的能力一起进化。在自家产品里，他们的做法是通过 sandboxing 来设这些边界，把任何潜在破坏性操作的影响范围限制住——对所有现在正在构建 tool-using agent 的人来说，这是一个非常实用的参考框架 ([链接](https://x.com/AnthropicAI/status/2059351260243919269))。
