### Swyx, Cognition and Latent Space podcast host

Swyx praised DeepSeek v4 for showing "utter confidence and competence by not benchmaxxing": no obsession with final-run cost, no inference-optimal compute games — just SOTA long-context efficiency techniques (CSA, HCA, mHC, flash at 8% the cost of pro, which itself is 14% of opus) and the best open base models in the world, with post-training left to the agent labs to pick up the scraps. He also said not enough people are experimenting with weird usages and finetunes of the base models the community does get, and pointed to Eugene's Latent Space Paper Club coverage of the release. ([tweet 1](https://x.com/swyx/status/2049569820728078533), [tweet 2](https://x.com/swyx/status/2049643473339601388), [tweet 3](https://x.com/swyx/status/2049572082317771179))

Swyx 力赞 DeepSeek v4 展现出"不刷榜的彻底自信与实力"：不纠结最终训练成本，也不玩 inference-optimal compute 那一套，直接交付 SOTA 的长上下文效率技术（CSA、HCA、mHC，flash 成本只有 pro 的 8%，而 pro 本身又只有 opus 的 14%），并放出全球最强的开源 base model，post-training 留给各家 agent 实验室自行去做。他还感叹，社区里少有人去玩这些 base model 的奇特用法和 finetune，并推荐了 Eugene 在 Latent Space Paper Club 的解读。([推文 1](https://x.com/swyx/status/2049569820728078533), [推文 2](https://x.com/swyx/status/2049643473339601388), [推文 3](https://x.com/swyx/status/2049572082317771179))

### Box CEO Aaron Levie

Levie wrote a widely shared post arguing software jobs aren't going away — agents are the single biggest leverage in history for anyone technical. His core claim: most of the world's software has not yet been built, and we're about to see "100x more software than before" — apps that need to get better, legacy on-prem systems waiting to be replatformed, SMBs that never could hire developers, security holes about to be uncovered, IT workflows that were never automatable, and untapped data inside most organizations. Every one of those agents will need a (technical) human to kick them off, manage them, orchestrate them, and turn their output into something useful. ([tweet](https://x.com/levie/status/2049333853777764495))

Box CEO Aaron Levie 发布了一条广为流传的观点：软件岗位不会消失——对任何懂技术的人来说，agent 是历史上最大的杠杆。他的核心判断是：全球大部分软件其实还没被造出来，我们即将迎来"比以往多 100 倍的软件"——还有大量需要变得更好的 app、等待迁云的本地遗留系统、从未雇得起工程师的中小企业、即将被发现的安全漏洞、过去无法自动化的 IT 工作流，以及大多数组织里尚未被处理和打通的数据。每一个 agent 都需要一个（懂技术的）人去启动它、管理它、编排它，并把它的输出变成可用的成果。([推文](https://x.com/levie/status/2049333853777764495))

### Every CEO Dan Shipper

Shipper interviewed Stripe's head of data and AI, Emily Glassberg Sands, on Every's AI & I podcast. Stripe sees roughly 2% of global GDP, giving it the best view of the early agent economy. Highlights: most people still don't trust AI with larger online purchases (a "superhero outfit for a kid who needs one stat" gets the green light, a vacation or a couch doesn't); fraud is moving up the stack, with stolen credit cards giving way to stolen free-trial tokens and compute credits — free-trial abuse has 4x'd in six months; AI is on both sides of fraud, and Stripe is blocking 250,000 fraudulent free trials a week for a single large customer; AI companies are growing faster than any cohort Stripe has ever tracked, with top names hitting $30M ARR in 18 months — 3x faster than the 2018 SaaS class — and so far it looks like net-new spend rather than cannibalized software budgets. Stripe is also rolling out a consumer wallet called Link for delegated agent purchases. ([tweet](https://x.com/danshipper/status/2049512129846530086))

Every CEO Dan Shipper 在 Every 的 AI & I 播客中采访了 Stripe 数据与 AI 负责人 Emily Glassberg Sands。Stripe 处理着全球约 2% 的 GDP 流量，因此对早期 agent 经济有最清晰的视角。重点包括：大多数人依然不太信任 AI 替自己做大额线上消费（"小孩急需的超人套装"可以让 agent 出手，度假或买沙发就不行）；欺诈正在沿技术栈向上迁移，从盗刷信用卡演变为盗用免费试用 token 和算力额度——过去半年免费试用滥用增长了 4 倍；AI 同时出现在欺诈的双方，Stripe 仅为一家大客户每周就拦截 25 万次欺诈性的免费试用；AI 公司的增长速度超过 Stripe 历史上跟踪过的任何一批客户，头部公司用 18 个月就做到 $30M ARR——比 2018 年的 SaaS 队列快 3 倍——目前来看更像是新增预算而不是吞噬已有的软件预算。Stripe 还在推出一款面向消费者的钱包 Link，用于授权 agent 完成代购。([推文](https://x.com/danshipper/status/2049512129846530086))

### South Park Commons GP Aditya Agarwal

Agarwal shared a blunt set of observations from heavy hands-on use of OpenClaw, Hermes and similar systems: most people who rave about these tools haven't actually used them; agents today are developer products, "so so so far" from being consumer-grade in delight, simplicity or reliability; he wants agents to generate dynamic, interactive interfaces instead of chat — he expects this to be the next big innovation vector; agents are fundamentally a wrapper around code generation and tool calling, not "generative AI" in the chat sense; once you internalize that core loop, old-school chat AI stops making sense — an always-on agent that adapts, learns and maintains state is far more compelling than a one-off prompt; and installing and running OpenClaw today feels like running Linux in the early 2000s — lots of jerry-rigging device drivers and configs, real sense of accomplishment, real lot of work to get to utility. ([tweet](https://x.com/adityaag/status/2049530878083379427))

South Park Commons GP Aditya Agarwal 分享了一组来自亲自折腾 OpenClaw、Hermes 等系统后的直白观察：很多对这些工具赞不绝口的人其实根本没真正用过；今天的 agent 还是开发者产品，距离"消费者级"的体验、简洁度和可靠性"非常非常非常"远；他希望 agent 能生成动态、可交互的界面，而不是聊天框——这很可能是下一波创新方向；agent 本质上是 code generation 和 tool calling 的封装，而不是聊天意义上的"generative AI"；一旦真正理解了这个核心循环，老式 chat AI 就显得没意思了——一个能持续运行、能适应、能学习、能维持状态的 agent 比一次性 prompt 有意思得多；今天装一套 OpenClaw 跑起来，感觉就像 2000 年代初装 Linux——大量时间花在编译驱动和配置上，能让你有强烈的成就感，但要真正用起来还是要付出大量工作。([推文](https://x.com/adityaag/status/2049530878083379427))

### Anthropic

Anthropic published BioMysteryBench, a new bioinformatics eval that tests whether Claude can devise creative solutions to open-ended research problems. Across 99 problems analyzing real biological data, an expert panel was stumped on 23 — Anthropic's most recent models solved roughly 30% of those, and most of the rest. Separately, new research from the Anthropic Fellows program introduced "introspection adapters," a tool that lets language models self-report behaviors they've learned during training, including potential misalignment. ([tweet 1](https://x.com/AnthropicAI/status/2049624600741560340), [tweet 2](https://x.com/AnthropicAI/status/2049624602486383078), [tweet 3](https://x.com/AnthropicAI/status/2049576143653929153))

Anthropic 发布了 BioMysteryBench，一个新的生物信息学评测集，用来测试 Claude 是否能为开放式研究问题想出有创造性的解决方案。在 99 道分析真实生物数据的题目中，由专家组成的小组在其中 23 道上"卡壳"——Anthropic 最新的模型解决了其中约 30%，并且解决了大部分剩余题目。另外，Anthropic Fellows 项目的新研究提出了"introspection adapters"——一种让语言模型自报告其在训练过程中学到的行为（包括潜在 misalignment）的工具。([推文 1](https://x.com/AnthropicAI/status/2049624600741560340), [推文 2](https://x.com/AnthropicAI/status/2049624602486383078), [推文 3](https://x.com/AnthropicAI/status/2049576143653929153))
