### Roblox PM and AI creator Peter Yang

Peter Yang floated a fun way to benchmark AI coding ability: which era of video game can a model one-shot a single level for (or build with 30 minutes of work)? His scale runs Atari → NES → SNES/Genesis → PlayStation/N64 → PS2/Xbox/GameCube → Xbox 360/PS3/Wii → PS4/Xbox One/Switch → PS5/Xbox Series. His read on where we are right now: still in the NES era. ([tweet](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2049232534064734331)) He also passed along Tibo's mindset shift for solo AI founders who are stuck trying to make one product work — Tibo shipped a new product a week, posted the landing page on Twitter, and moved on if it didn't get traction. Nine products failed; the tenth took off. The takeaway: treat every release as an experiment, ship early and often, don't try to make it perfect. ([tweet](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2049136158756733237))

Roblox 产品负责人、AI 内容创作者 Peter Yang 提出一个挺有意思的 AI 编码能力 benchmark：模型能一次过通关哪个年代的游戏（或者用 30 分钟做出来）？他给出的标尺从 Atari → NES → SNES/Genesis → PlayStation/N64 → PS2/Xbox/GameCube → Xbox 360/PS3/Wii → PS4/Xbox One/Switch → PS5/Xbox Series 一路往上排，而他对当下 AI 水平的判断是：还停留在 NES 时代。([推文](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2049232534064734331)) 他还转述了 Tibo 给那些"卡在一个产品上反复折腾"的独立 AI 创业者的一条心法——Tibo 那时每周发一个新产品，做个 landing page 发到 Twitter 上，没人理就直接换下一个，连续 9 个失败，第 10 个才真正起飞。结论很直接：把每次发布当作一次实验，频繁发布，不要追求完美。([推文](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2049136158756733237))

### Replit CEO Amjad Masad

Amjad Masad weighed in on GitHub buckling under bot-driven traffic, saying it's actually impressive the service has stayed up at all. His prediction from years ago: free services become untenable once you have human-level bots, and the obvious lever to pull is micro-payments — even cents per git push could reduce spam and make things sustainable. He'd like to see Bitcoin powering it so the system stays open and avoids KYCing every user. ([tweet](https://x.com/amasad/status/2049242460078100638))

Replit CEO Amjad Masad 谈了 GitHub 在 bot 流量冲击下的处境：他觉得服务还能撑住已经很厉害了。他几年前就预测过：一旦"接近人类水平"的 bot 普及，免费服务就会撑不下去，最直接的对策是引入 micro-payments——哪怕每次 git push 收几分钱，也足以压制 spam、让服务可持续。他更倾向于用 Bitcoin 来承载这层支付，这样能保持开放、避免对所有用户做 KYC。([推文](https://x.com/amasad/status/2049242460078100638))

### Box CEO Aaron Levie

Aaron Levie laid out who agentic coding is and isn't for. It's a huge boon for software developers who want to ship more, for IT folks building custom internal systems, for domain experts automating workflows, and for anyone curious to learn coding. What it's *less* great for: casually building complex software you then have to maintain — upgrades, security patches, ongoing care are taxes most knowledge workers aren't prepared to pay. His net take: we'll get 100x more software and vastly more developers, but that's different from *everyone* rolling their own. ([tweet](https://x.com/levie/status/2049163935182733396))

Box CEO Aaron Levie 把 agentic coding 的适用边界讲得很清楚。它对想多产出的软件工程师、想自建内部系统的 IT 团队、想把工作流自动化的领域专家、以及任何对学编程有好奇心的人来说，都是巨大的红利。但它*不*适合的场景是：随手做出一个你还得长期维护的复杂软件——升级、打补丁、跟进最新的安全漏洞，这些都是大多数知识工作者没准备好承担的"税"。他的结论是：未来会出现 100 倍的软件、远比现在更多的开发者，但这不等于"人人都自己搓一套"。([推文](https://x.com/levie/status/2049163935182733396))

### Every CEO Dan Shipper

Dan Shipper has been using PostHog inside Codex and says it's dramatically better than browsing PostHog separately — Codex can write queries and see the results, then kick off agents that write PRs or run prod DB requests to back up the insights it finds. His framing: a browser inside your desktop coding orchestration tool beats an agent inside your browser. ([tweet](https://x.com/danshipper/status/2049236793761976357)) He's also coining a category — Codex-native, Cowork-native, Cursor-native — for apps designed to live inside the in-app browser of your agent of choice, where the agent and the human share full context and can both see what the other is doing. He sees a big greenfield to build here. ([tweet](https://x.com/danshipper/status/2049223933766041688))

Every CEO Dan Shipper 一直在 Codex 里用 PostHog，他觉得这种用法比单独开 PostHog 网页强太多——Codex 能写查询、能看到结果，还能进一步派 agent 去开 PR 或者跑生产数据库请求来佐证它发现的洞察。他的总结是：把浏览器塞进你的桌面 coding orchestration 工具里，比把 agent 塞进你的浏览器里更好用。([推文](https://x.com/danshipper/status/2049236793761976357)) 他还顺势造了一类新词——Codex-native、Cowork-native、Cursor-native——指那些专门为"运行在你常用 agent 的内置浏览器里"而设计的应用，agent 和人共享全部上下文，彼此都能看到对方在干什么。他认为这是一片巨大的待开发地带。([推文](https://x.com/danshipper/status/2049223933766041688))

### Google DeepMind

Google DeepMind expanded its Experience AI education program — built with the Raspberry Pi Foundation — into Latin America, backed by $4.6M in funding from Google.org. The plan is to train 24,000 educators and reach 1.25M students by 2028. ([tweet](https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2049174334057611585)) The numbers so far: 30,000+ teachers trained, 2.9M students reached across 180 countries in 19 languages, with 93% of educators saying the training increased their AI knowledge and 87% feeling more confident teaching the topics. ([tweet](https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2049174330064470022)) ([tweet](https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2049174327124263379))

Google DeepMind 把它与 Raspberry Pi Foundation 合作的 Experience AI 教育项目扩展到了拉丁美洲，由 Google.org 提供 460 万美元资金支持，目标是在 2028 年前培训 24,000 名教师、覆盖 125 万名学生。([推文](https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2049174334057611585)) 截至目前，该项目已培训超过 30,000 名教师，触达 180 个国家、19 种语言下的 290 万名学生；93% 的教师表示培训增进了他们对 AI 的理解，87% 表示在讲解这些复杂话题时更有信心。([推文](https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2049174330064470022)) ([推文](https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2049174327124263379))
