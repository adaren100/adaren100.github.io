### Latent Space's Swyx, AI Engineer organizer

Latent Space's Swyx (also affiliated with Cognition) made a sharp framing call: every evals/analytics startup in 2026 is going through a one-time generational upgrade into a continual learning platform. Many will fail, but as always the tasteful ones win ([link](https://x.com/swyx/status/2061206120233054327)).

Latent Space 的 Swyx（同时挂着 Cognition 的帽子）抛出了一句很锋利的定调：2026 年所有 evals/analytics 创业公司都在经历一次性的代际升级，往 continual learning platform 这个方向迁。大部分会死，但和过去一样，最后赢的是那批"有品味"的团队 ([链接](https://x.com/swyx/status/2061206120233054327))。

### Roblox Product lead Peter Yang

Roblox Product lead Peter Yang's favorite skill of the week is Josh Pigford's `/but-for-real` skill, which forces AI to double-check its own work with brutally honest commentary — classic lines like "you just mass-produced a pile of changes with the unearned confidence of a junior dev who's never had a production incident" and a reframe that "can you try again?" is just polite code for "you failed" ([link](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2061171582383563222)). He also riffed on Pigford's prompt-engineering tip — "pretend your worst enemy wrote this code" — as a surprisingly effective code-review framing ([link](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2061173405366571251)), and posted a satirical "how to win in AI" list skewering 996/tokenmax/Forbes 30 under 30 culture ([link](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2061175114184736792)).

Roblox 的产品负责人 Peter Yang 这周最喜欢的 skill 是 Josh Pigford 做的 `/but-for-real`——它逼着 AI 用一种毒舌但诚实的口吻 review 自己的工作，经典句子比如"你刚刚以一种没经历过线上事故的初级工程师的迷之自信，量产了一堆改动"，还有一句翻译："can you try again?"其实是用户太礼貌不好意思直说"你搞砸了"的客气版 ([链接](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2061171582383563222))。他也转了 Pigford 另一条 prompt 技巧——"假装这段代码是你最讨厌的那个人写的"——作为一种效果意外好的 code review 框架 ([链接](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2061173405366571251))；另外他写了一份讽刺向的"如何在 AI 时代赢"清单，把 996、tokenmax、Forbes 30 under 30 这套文化挨个调侃了一遍 ([链接](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2061175114184736792))。

### Vercel CEO Guillermo Rauch

Vercel CEO Guillermo Rauch shared a striking observation from his DMs: public company CEOs and CTOs are coding again, with a fury, because of coding agents like Claude Code and Vercel's stack. He calls coding agents "the ultimate PLG-fication of the enterprise" — bad, legacy software can no longer hide once the entire org, from intern to CEO, can directly feel which stack works ([link](https://x.com/rauchg/status/2061135404942974982)).

Vercel CEO Guillermo Rauch 抛了一个很值得玩味的观察：上市公司的 CEO 和 CTO 在被 coding agent 拉回到亲手写代码这件事上——他的 DM 和 InMail 里塞满了这类高管，说自己因为 Claude Code 和 Vercel 那套东西重新爱上了发版。他的总结是：coding agent 是 enterprise 的终极 PLG 化——烂的、legacy 的软件再也藏不住了，因为从实习生到 CEO 整个组织都能直接感受到哪套 stack 是真的能跑 ([链接](https://x.com/rauchg/status/2061135404942974982))。

### Box CEO Aaron Levie

Box CEO Aaron Levie pushed back on the "AI replaces jobs" narrative with on-the-ground data from his CIO/CTO/CEO conversations: most large enterprises are either net-growing thanks to AI (new FDE and engineering functions) or reinvesting efficiency savings back into sales, marketing, and customer success. Citing Goldman's David Solomon, he argues businesses have always been constrained by how much software, outbound, or risk coverage they can afford — and AI loosens all of those constraints simultaneously, so the dollars get redeployed instead of cut. His framing: companies that just chase savings lose; companies that better serve customers with the new capacity win ([link](https://x.com/levie/status/2060923684295221390)).

Box CEO Aaron Levie 用一线数据反驳了"AI 替代工作"那套叙事：在他和大企业 CIO/CTO/CEO 的对话里，大多数公司要么因为 AI 在净增长（新增 FDE、新的工程职能），要么至少是把效率节省下来的钱重新投回 sales、marketing、customer success。他引用 Goldman Sachs CEO David Solomon 上周在 NYTimes 的 OpEd 论证：企业一直被"我能写多少软件、雇多少销售、跑多少 marketing campaign、做多精细的 outbound、覆盖多少风险"这些预算硬约束卡住——AI 把这些约束同时松开了，所以钱是被重新部署而不是砍掉。他的判断是：只想用 AI 砍成本的公司会输，用 AI 把客户服务得更好的公司才会长期赢 ([链接](https://x.com/levie/status/2060923684295221390))。

### Y Combinator CEO Garry Tan

Y Combinator CEO Garry Tan made a sharp prediction: memory is the defining battlefield of "the AI harness wars of 2027." You should want to control and host your own memory, because it's the one thing you should be able to carry across any platform — otherwise you're sharecropping someone else's AI ecosystem ([link](https://x.com/garrytan/status/2061174413513678941), [link](https://x.com/garrytan/status/2061176075288453333)). He's also openly musing about shipping "gskillpacks" ([link](https://x.com/garrytan/status/2061173908917846040)).

Y Combinator CEO Garry Tan 抛出了一个很锋利的预测：memory 是"2027 年 AI harness 之战"真正的主战场。他认为 memory 是你最应该自己掌控和自己 host 的东西，因为它是你唯一应该能带去任何平台的资产——否则你只是在别人家的 AI 生态里当佃农 ([链接](https://x.com/garrytan/status/2061174413513678941)、[链接](https://x.com/garrytan/status/2061176075288453333))。他还半公开地在琢磨要不要做一套 "gskillpacks" ([链接](https://x.com/garrytan/status/2061173908917846040))。

### FPV Ventures partner Nikunj Kothari

FPV Ventures partner Nikunj Kothari put out an open ask for actual statistical studies on which jobs AI has *meaningfully* replaced — historical baselines, current openings, forward-looking statements. His take, in spite of all the layoff and "model revenue booming" headlines: the risk of a permanent AI underclass feels wildly overblown, and he wants harder data to either confirm or kill that intuition ([link](https://x.com/nikunj/status/2061115431528943775)).

FPV Ventures 的合伙人 Nikunj Kothari 公开求助：有没有真正像样的统计研究，能说明 AI 到底"实质性地"替代了哪些工作——历史基线、当前在招、以及前瞻性的预期。他自己的判断是，尽管裁员新闻和模型公司收入暴涨的标题铺天盖地，"AI 永久 underclass"这个风险其实被显著夸大了，但他想要更硬的数据来证实或证伪这个直觉 ([链接](https://x.com/nikunj/status/2061115431528943775))。

### OpenClaw maintainer Peter Steinberger

OpenClaw maintainer Peter Steinberger has been teaching Codex to act as his QA assistant: for every commit, Codex generates a user-test scenario and then drives webVNC (crabbox) plus computer/browser-use tooling (peekaboo/mcporter) to test OpenClaw the way a real QA person would — running in the background and opening PRs with fixes ([link](https://x.com/steipete/status/2061208638027395490)). He was also impressed to see Codex write ad-hoc codemods on its own for a sizable TypeScript migration ([link](https://x.com/steipete/status/2061115471760441692)), and reiterated OpenClaw's design philosophy: modular and lean, add only what you need, because fewer skills and fewer tools let the agent work more efficiently ([link](https://x.com/steipete/status/2061072753998856696)).

OpenClaw 的维护者 Peter Steinberger 最近在把 Codex 调教成自己的 QA：每次 commit，Codex 会生成一个用户测试场景，然后通过 webVNC（crabbox）加 computer/browser use 工具（peekaboo/mcporter）像真正的 QA 一样去跑 OpenClaw——整个流程在后台跑，发现问题直接开 PR 修 ([链接](https://x.com/steipete/status/2061208638027395490))。他还被 Codex 的一手操作震到：在一次较大规模的 TypeScript 迁移里，Codex 自己写了 ad-hoc codemod ([链接](https://x.com/steipete/status/2061115471760441692))。同时他重申了 OpenClaw 的设计哲学：模块化、精简，只加你真正需要的东西，因为 skill 越少、tool 越少，agent 跑得才越高效 ([链接](https://x.com/steipete/status/2061072753998856696))。

### OpenAI CEO Sam Altman

OpenAI CEO Sam Altman announced OpenAI Robotics is now hiring full-stack hardware, ops, systems, and ML engineers. The team is the evolved form of OpenAI's world simulation research program (led by Aditya Ramesh) and is built on tight co-design between robotics hardware and ML research. Short-term focus: robots that support skilled workers building infrastructure. Long-term vision: a personal robot for everyone ([link](https://x.com/sama/status/2061117302528188712)). Separately, he flagged OpenAI's new biodefense push as wanting to give the world a head start on the problem ([link](https://x.com/sama/status/2061101875303530871)).

OpenAI CEO Sam Altman 宣布 OpenAI Robotics 正式开招 full-stack hardware、ops、systems 和 ML engineer。这个团队是从过去一年由 Aditya Ramesh 带的 world simulation 研究项目演化而来，核心方法论是 robotics 硬件和 ML 研究的紧密 co-design。短期目标：做能帮"建造未来基础设施"的熟练工人那种 robot；长期目标：每个人都拥有一个能搞定任何事的私人 robot ([链接](https://x.com/sama/status/2061117302528188712))。另外他还点了一下 OpenAI 在 biodefense 上的新动作，定位是"想让世界在这个问题上提前一步" ([链接](https://x.com/sama/status/2061101875303530871))。
