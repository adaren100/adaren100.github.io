### Roblox Product Lead Peter Yang

Peter Yang relayed sharp quotes from Dario and Daniela Amodei at Code with Claude. Anthropic saw 80x growth in usage and revenue earlier this year, which is why they're aggressively acquiring compute. Dario's framing for builders: "Build for the exponential. There are products that aren't possible with the current model but could work with later models," which puts a premium on internal experimentation — you have to actually try it with the model to know. He also noted today's model gains show up much more clearly in agentic and Claude Code workflows than in chatbots, and that models are evolving "from coding to software engineering to just growing a business." (https://x.com/petergyang/status/2052123472583864780, https://x.com/petergyang/status/2052117599744672195, https://x.com/petergyang/status/2052177418555977866)

Roblox 产品负责人 Peter Yang 把 Code with Claude 现场 Dario 和 Daniela Amodei 的几句犀利发言带了回来。Anthropic 今年早些时候在使用量和收入上都翻了 80 倍，这也是他们在拼命囤算力的原因。Dario 给 builder 的建议是："要按指数曲线去设计产品。有些东西现在的模型做不到，但后面的模型一定能做到，"所以内部的提前实验非常关键——你必须真的拿模型上手试，才能知道行不行。他还说，现在模型能力的提升在 agent 和 Claude Code 这类工作流里体现得比 chatbot 明显得多——模型正在"从写代码、做软件工程，进化到直接经营一门生意"。(https://x.com/petergyang/status/2052123472583864780, https://x.com/petergyang/status/2052117599744672195, https://x.com/petergyang/status/2052177418555977866)

### Replit CEO Amjad Masad

Replit CEO Amjad Masad pushed back hard against a security firm called Red Access, which gave Replit less than 24 hours before going to the press. His point: standard responsible-disclosure practice (CISA, CERT/CC, most major firms) is private notification with a defined remediation window, not a press countdown. The substance of Red Access's claim, as he understood it, was that some users had published apps publicly that should have been private — but Replit's design lets users explicitly choose public vs private, and public apps being on the open web is expected behavior. He used the moment to point at two security products Replit shipped this past week, Security Agent and Auto-Protect, and offered to proactively flip impacted apps to private and notify users if Red Access shares the list. (https://x.com/amasad/status/2051847991125049569)

Replit CEO Amjad Masad 强硬回击了一家叫 Red Access 的安全公司——对方只给了 Replit 不到 24 小时就把消息送给了媒体。他的观点很直接：行业里负责任披露的标准做法（CISA、CERT/CC、绝大多数大公司）是先私下通知，给一个明确的修复窗口，而不是用新闻倒计时来逼人。就 Red Access 那个所谓"漏洞"的实质，他的理解是：有些用户把本应设为私有的 app 发到了公网——但 Replit 本身就让用户自己选择 public 或 private，public app 跑在公开网络上完全是预期行为。他顺便提到 Replit 上周刚上线的两个安全产品：Security Agent 和 Auto-Protect，并表示如果 Red Access 愿意提供受影响用户名单，他们会主动把那些 app 切到 private 并通知用户。(https://x.com/amasad/status/2051847991125049569)

Every CEO Dan Shipper made a strong claim: three months ago Codex was "trash for knowledge work," and now it's his daily driver — for writing, recruiting, deep engineering, even keeping him at inbox zero. He interviewed Every's head of growth Austin Tedesco about why Austin now spends ~80% of his working time inside the Codex desktop app. The pitch: the desktop app is faster and more reliable than Claude Desktop / Cowork, and that's the real differentiator. ### South Park Commons Partner Aditya Agarwal

Aditya Agarwal's provocative take on the venture market: the hardest spot to be in right now is a Series A/B company at $10–20M ARR with 20–50 employees but growing under 25%. His framing — that's "ngmi." You don't iterate your way out of it; the only real shot is to "return back to Minus One": figure out the core assets you actually have, and use them as the basis for a much bigger swing. He admits most founders won't have the courage and fortitude to make that pivot, but the alternative is a slow, painful decline. (https://x.com/adityaag/status/2052075380442607877)

South Park Commons 合伙人 Aditya Agarwal 给出了一个挑衅式判断：venture 圈里现在最难的位置，是那种 Series A/B 阶段、ARR 在 1000 万到 2000 万美元、员工 20–50 人、但增速却低于 25% 的公司。他直接说这是"ngmi"。这种局面靠迭代是迭代不出来的；真正的出路是"回到 Minus One"——盘点自己手里真正的核心资产，再以此为底押一把更大的赌注。他也承认大部分创始人没有这个胆量和耐力去做这种转弯，但另一条路就是慢慢、痛苦地死掉。(https://x.com/adityaag/status/2052075380442607877)
