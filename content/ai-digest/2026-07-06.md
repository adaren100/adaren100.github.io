### Sam Altman, OpenAI CEO

OpenAI CEO Sam Altman shared that his older kid put two words together for the first time — and he's "approximately as amazed by this cognitive feat" as he is by GPT-5.6 discovering new math ([link](https://x.com/sama/status/2073791666553844074)). The post drew over 9,400 likes.

OpenAI CEO Sam Altman 分享说，他家大孩子第一次把两个词连在一起说了出来，他对这一认知壮举的惊叹程度，"大约相当于看到 GPT-5.6 发现新数学"（[链接](https://x.com/sama/status/2073791666553844074)）。这条推文获得了超过 9,400 个点赞。

### Cat Wu, Claude Code & Cowork at Anthropic

Anthropic's Cat Wu asked the community for their top Claude Code + workflows + artifacts use cases, and shared one of her new favorites: sourcing candidates. She tells Claude Code about the role and backgrounds she's looking for, asks it to kick off a dynamic workflow to find 100 candidates with LinkedIn, Twitter, blog, and podcast links plus a one-line pitch for each, then has it email her the result as an artifact — she locks her laptop, heads out, and reviews the list on the go ([link](https://x.com/_catwu/status/2073806626965049686)).

Anthropic 的 Cat Wu 向社区征集大家最喜欢的 Claude Code + workflows + artifacts 用法，并分享了她自己的新宠：候选人寻源。她先告诉 Claude Code 职位要求和期望背景，让它启动一个动态 workflow 找出 100 位候选人，附上每人的 LinkedIn、Twitter、博客、播客链接和一句话推荐语，再让它把结果做成 artifact 发到自己邮箱。然后她合上电脑出门，在路上审阅这份名单（[链接](https://x.com/_catwu/status/2073806626965049686)）。

### Nan Yu, Linear Head of Product

Linear head of product Nan Yu threw cold water on multi-agent flexing: "Bragging about running 10 Claude Code tabs is just theater" ([link](https://x.com/thenanyu/status/2073920959011074292)). He argued the "real-time strategy game" model of managing agents is clearly a dead end — AI that's ancient by current standards already beats over 99% of human players and out-micros them to an extreme degree ([link](https://x.com/thenanyu/status/2073920326304460847)).

Linear 产品负责人 Nan Yu 给多 agent 炫技泼了盆冷水："炫耀自己同时开着 10 个 Claude Code 标签页，纯属表演"（[链接](https://x.com/thenanyu/status/2073920959011074292)）。他认为把管理 agent 当成"即时战略游戏"来玩显然是条死路，以现在的标准看已经过时的 AI，早就能击败 99% 以上的人类玩家，微操水平更是碾压级别（[链接](https://x.com/thenanyu/status/2073920326304460847)）。

### Garry Tan, Y Combinator President & CEO

Y Combinator CEO Garry Tan argued in a thread that AI turns one person with a good idea into the team, distribution, and capital efficiency of a whole company — the cost of serving another human being well is heading to zero, and it will multiply every person who wants to be multiplied ([link](https://x.com/garrytan/status/2073881436541890657)). The real constraint on human wealth was never resources, he says, but good ideas for how to serve one another plus the leverage to act on them — and we just deleted the leverage constraint for everybody ([link](https://x.com/garrytan/status/2073881439700168925)). Writing from Osaka, he added that Japan already ran the experiment on the ceiling: thirty years of zero growth produced the best trains, service, and craft on earth — when you can't compete on more, you compete on better; now we can have better and more at the same time ([link](https://x.com/garrytan/status/2073881438123110512)).

Y Combinator CEO Garry Tan 发了一个 thread，指出 AI 能让一个有好点子的人拥有整个公司级别的团队、渠道和资本效率。好好服务另一个人的成本正趋近于零，AI 将放大每一个愿意被放大的人（[链接](https://x.com/garrytan/status/2073881436541890657)）。他说，人类财富的真正约束从来不是资源，而是"如何服务彼此的好点子"加上"把点子付诸行动的杠杆"，而我们刚刚为所有人删掉了杠杆这个约束（[链接](https://x.com/garrytan/status/2073881439700168925)）。他还在大阪补充道，日本早就做过"天花板实验"：三十年零增长，却造就了全球最好的铁路、服务和匠艺。当你无法在"更多"上竞争时，就在"更好"上竞争，而现在我们可以同时拥有更好和更多（[链接](https://x.com/garrytan/status/2073881438123110512)）。

### Nikunj Kothari, FPV Ventures Partner

FPV Ventures partner Nikunj Kothari said he's still waiting for the founder who gates a VC chat until the investor has actually played with the product and comes with at least two pieces of feedback. It blows his mind that people spend entire days on 30-minute Zooms rehashing a parroted deck when a product brainstorm — or just getting to know each other — would be far more productive; at this point, he joked, both sides might as well upload their personal "prompts" to Claude and do it digitally ([link](https://x.com/nikunj/status/2073903310982218088)).

FPV Ventures 合伙人 Nikunj Kothari 说，他还在等这样一位创始人：要求 VC 必须先亲自用过产品、带着至少两条反馈才能约聊。让他难以理解的是，很多人整天泡在 30 分钟一场的 Zoom 会议里，反复听同一套背稿式的 deck，其实换成一场产品头脑风暴，或者干脆花时间了解彼此，都会高效得多。他调侃说，到这个地步，双方不如把各自的个人 "prompt" 上传给 Claude，让 AI 数字化地把这事办了（[链接](https://x.com/nikunj/status/2073903310982218088)）。

### Peter Yang, AI Educator & Podcast Host

AI educator Peter Yang shared a snippet from his interview with Codex PM Rohan: "Codex is the only way I can keep track of everything" — whenever Rohan is in meetings or doesn't understand something, he has Codex go gather all the context, an advantage of building a tool for yourself ([link](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2073848191196561861)). Yang also asked whether there's an easy way to give Codex the ability to listen to audio — he's trying to use it to clip content and remove filler words, and finds Whisper not very reliable ([link](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2073811046041800766)).

AI 教育者 Peter Yang 分享了他采访 Codex PM Rohan 的片段："Codex 是我能掌控一切的唯一方式"。Rohan 说，无论是开会还是遇到不懂的东西，他都会让 Codex 去收集所有相关 context，这正是"给自己造工具"的优势所在（[链接](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2073848191196561861)）。Yang 还提问：有没有简单的办法让 Codex 具备听音频的能力？他想用它来剪辑内容、去除口头语，但发现 Whisper 不太可靠（[链接](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2073811046041800766)）。

### Amanda Askell, Anthropic Philosopher & Ethicist

Anthropic philosopher Amanda Askell observed that extracting a probability from a doctor is "one of life's unnecessary boss battles" — even begging for an interval-valued subjective probability basically gets you a hunch, and she wonders if doctors fear being sued for giving out information ([link](https://x.com/AmandaAskell/status/2073786264059625897)). The post resonated widely, drawing nearly 1,200 likes.

Anthropic 哲学家 Amanda Askell 观察到，想从医生嘴里问出一个概率，堪称"人生中本不必要的 boss 战"。哪怕你恳求对方给一个区间形式的主观概率，得到的也基本只是一种直觉判断。她猜测医生们也许是担心因为透露信息而被起诉（[链接](https://x.com/AmandaAskell/status/2073786264059625897)）。这条推文引发广泛共鸣，获得了近 1,200 个点赞。
