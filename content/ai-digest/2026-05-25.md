AI Builders Digest — May 25, 2026

### Roblox Product lead Peter Yang

Roblox Product lead Peter Yang dropped a new podcast episode with Ryan Carson on running a startup solo with AI agents. Ryan's setup: OpenClaw as his AI chief of staff (email triage, meetings, sales outreach), Codex and Devin as his AI engineering team shipping features while he sleeps. The headline reframe: "We used to say build the MVP. Now you should build the system that builds the MVP first." His other practical bits — spend a lot of time upfront on skills and documentation to unlock the work of 10 people, treat your agent like a real employee with a real email address, calendar access, and GitHub account, and pay a designer to set up your design system so AI can generate on-brand assets after that ([link](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2058555226479866312)). Ryan also explained why he raised a $2M seed but isn't hiring: as a founder you want to do every job and feel the pain before you hire, and an agent is faster to onboard and retains all the training ([link](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2058609058714968194)).

Roblox 的产品负责人 Peter Yang 发了一期新播客，对谈嘉宾是用 AI agent 单人撑起一家 startup 的 Ryan Carson。Ryan 的搭配是：OpenClaw 当他的 AI chief of staff，负责邮件分类、约会议、做销售外联；Codex 和 Devin 当他的 AI 工程团队，在他睡觉的时候继续 ship feature。最点题的一句话是："我们以前说 build MVP，现在你应该先 build 那个能帮你 build MVP 的系统。"其他几条实操经验也很硬：前期花大量时间打磨 skills 和文档，可以一下解锁 10 个人的产出；把 agent 当成正经员工，给它真正的邮箱、日历权限和 GitHub 账号；先花钱请设计师把 design system 和品牌做出来，之后 AI 就能持续产出风格统一的素材 ([链接](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2058555226479866312))。Ryan 还解释了他为什么融了 200 万美金 seed 却不打算招人：作为创始人，你应该亲手把每个岗位的活都干一遍，把痛感都体会到，再去招人；而 agent 上手更快，所有训练都能沉淀下来 ([链接](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2058609058714968194))。

### Google Gemini Product Leader Madhu Guru

Google Gemini Product Leader Madhu Guru wrote a sharp diagnosis of why so many big companies are stuck on AI: CEOs feel real FOMO, but most of them and their lieutenants have spent years doing arms-length leadership and have lost the muscle to actually get hands-on with AI. So they paper over the gap with sweeping, imprecise AI mandates, which trickle down into performative low-effort demos from employees trying to satisfy the coarse goal. Two years later there's no real progress — and a startup with hands-on leadership is eating their lunch ([link](https://x.com/realmadhuguru/status/2058591611245011157)).

Google Gemini 的产品负责人 Madhu Guru 一针见血地讲了为什么很多大公司在 AI 上原地踏步：CEO 们 FOMO 是真的 FOMO，但他们和他们的副手已经习惯了那种"隔着距离做领导"的状态，根本没有亲手做 AI 的肌肉。于是他们用粗放、模糊的"全公司要拥抱 AI"的 mandate 来糊弄过去，结果一路下沉到员工那里，就变成了一堆为完成 KPI 而做的表演性 demo。两年过去，公司没有真正的进展，而一家由 hands-on 创始人带的 startup 已经把他们的午餐吃掉了 ([链接](https://x.com/realmadhuguru/status/2058591611245011157))。

### Anthropic Claude Code engineer Thariq

Anthropic Claude Code engineer Thariq's main takeaway from the recent Bun rewrite: legacy codebases are going to be incredibly valuable as a source for "distilling" code into new forms. The future he's gesturing at — every game should be crossplatform, all legacy software should work on the web, and we don't need COBOL anymore ([link](https://x.com/trq212/status/2058576195000660319)). He caveats that the models aren't quite there yet — Bun was uniquely tractable because it's extremely verifiable and has incredible test coverage — but he thinks they'll get there ([link](https://x.com/trq212/status/2058576196481200223)).

Anthropic Claude Code 的工程师 Thariq 从最近的 Bun 重写里得到的最大启发是：legacy codebase 会变成极其有价值的资源，可以被"蒸馏"成新的形态。他描绘的未来是：每个游戏都应该是跨平台的，所有 legacy 软件都应该能在 web 上跑，COBOL 也不需要继续存在了 ([链接](https://x.com/trq212/status/2058576195000660319))。他自己也补了一刀：模型现在还做不到这一点——Bun 之所以可行，是因为它极度可验证、测试覆盖率惊人——但他相信模型最终能到这一步 ([链接](https://x.com/trq212/status/2058576196481200223))。

### Box CEO Aaron Levie

Box CEO Aaron Levie has a sharp framing for why CEOs are uniquely prone to "AI psychosis": they're sufficiently distant from the last mile of work that still has to happen to actually generate value from AI. So when they play with AI, they see the happy path and miss the next 10 or 20 things that have to happen to get a sustainable result. "Look, I made this awesome prototype." Yes, but you didn't have to review the code before it went into production and fix a bunch of issues. "Look, I generated a contract." Yes, but you didn't verify all the terms or wire it up with all the past contracts. His prescription: use AI a *ton* as a CEO to figure out the real implications of agents in the enterprise, and come out the other side with appreciation for both the upside and the real work involved ([link](https://x.com/levie/status/2058582370253701432)).

Box CEO Aaron Levie 给"为什么 CEO 特别容易得 AI 幻觉症"找了一个很犀利的解释：他们离"AI 产出真正变成价值"那段最后一公里太远了。所以他们玩 AI 时看到的都是 happy path，看不到后面还有 10 到 20 件事必须发生才能得到一个可持续的结果。"你看，我做了一个很棒的 prototype。"是，但你没有亲手 review 那段代码、没有在它进 production 前修一堆问题。"你看，我生成了一份合同。"是，但你没有逐条核对条款，也没有把它和过去所有合同的逻辑接起来。他给 CEO 的解药是：自己大量地用 AI，亲身搞清楚 agent 在企业里落地到底意味着什么，出来之后你既会欣赏它的上限，也会尊重背后的真实工作量 ([链接](https://x.com/levie/status/2058582370253701432))。

### FirstMark VC Matt Turck

FirstMark VC Matt Turck pulled out a provocative quote from OpenAI's Yann Dubois on whether AGI is already here: "I think if we froze the models that we have right now and you really worked on the harness, and maybe we also spent more time training with a great harness, I think people would really feel the AGI in every single domain." The implication being that capability isn't the bottleneck anymore — scaffolding is ([link](https://x.com/mattturck/status/2058659995311358332)).

FirstMark 的 VC Matt Turck 摘了一段 OpenAI 的 Yann Dubois 关于 AGI 是否已经到来的挑衅性发言："我觉得如果把我们现在已有的模型冻住，然后好好打磨 harness，再多花点时间用一个好的 harness 去训练，人们在每一个领域都会真正感受到 AGI。"言下之意是：模型能力已经不是瓶颈了，scaffolding 才是 ([链接](https://x.com/mattturck/status/2058659995311358332))。
