### OpenAI

OpenAI shipped an upgraded GPT-5.5 Instant that's "much more fun to talk to," with better understanding of intent behind questions, more reliable handling of complex constraints, and more useful, cohesive shopping and local recommendations. It's rolling out today to paid users and tomorrow to free users ([link](https://x.com/OpenAI/status/2069843083701915755)). OpenAI also unveiled its first in-house AI chip, Jalapeño, designed from the ground up with Broadcom and purpose-built for the LLM workloads behind ChatGPT, Codex, the API, and future agentic products — part of a push to expand OpenAI's full-stack platform from products and models down to infrastructure ([link](https://x.com/OpenAI/status/2069770172802773292)).

OpenAI 发布了升级版 GPT-5.5 Instant，称其"聊起来更有趣"，能更好地理解问题背后的意图，更可靠地处理复杂约束条件，购物和本地推荐也更实用、更连贯。该版本今天向付费用户推出，明天向免费用户推出（[链接](https://x.com/OpenAI/status/2069843083701915755)）。OpenAI 还推出了自研的首款 AI 芯片 Jalapeño，与 Broadcom 联合从零设计，专为支撑 ChatGPT、Codex、API 以及未来 agentic 产品的 LLM 工作负载打造——这是 OpenAI 将全栈平台从产品、模型延伸到基础设施的重要一步（[链接](https://x.com/OpenAI/status/2069770172802773292)）。

### Aaron Levie, Box CEO

Box CEO Aaron Levie laid out a "barbell" dynamic in AI pricing — high-cost frontier models on one end, cheap-but-good open or closed weight models on the other — and argued the applied AI layer is where the value gets captured, by routing to the best model for a given workload and mitigating cost or performance tradeoffs. He says that requires deeply understanding workflows, domain-specific FDEs, customer/process-level evals, and well-structured data for agents ([link](https://x.com/levie/status/2069639600310767616)). He also highlighted Box's "Claude Tag" integration, which lets Claude access any corporate files in Box directly from Slack, turning enterprise content into a portable knowledge base ([link](https://x.com/levie/status/2069596515560267891)).

Box CEO Aaron Levie 提出了 AI 定价的"杠铃式"动态——一端是高成本的前沿模型，另一端是便宜但效果不错的开源或闭源权重模型——并认为应用 AI 层才是价值真正被捕获的地方，它能根据具体工作负载路由到最合适的模型，平衡成本和性能。他认为这需要深入理解工作流程、领域专属的 FDE、按客户/流程划分的 evals，以及为 agent 准备良好结构化的数据（[链接](https://x.com/levie/status/2069639600310767616)）。他还提到了 Box 的"Claude Tag"集成，能让 Claude 直接从 Slack 访问 Box 中的任意企业文件，把企业内容变成可移动的知识库（[链接](https://x.com/levie/status/2069596515560267891)）。

### Dan Shipper, Every CEO

Every CEO Dan Shipper interviewed Surge AI CEO Edwin Chen, who believes AI may soon do everything humans can. Shipper himself argues AI automation actually creates more human work and that we're farther from AI replacing humans than it seems. Key points from the conversation: LLMs have no intrinsic motivation or ability to set their own goals; models optimized for engagement don't produce the most valuable output (Chen described spending 20 rounds polishing a pointless email before Claude told him to just send it); and models trained on writing metrics tend to hack them, overusing devices like metaphor in every sentence ([link](https://x.com/danshipper/status/2069805581263847467)).

Every CEO Dan Shipper采访了 Surge AI CEO Edwin Chen，后者认为 AI 可能很快就能做到人类能做的一切。Shipper 本人则认为 AI 自动化实际上会创造更多人类工作，我们离 AI 取代人类还有相当距离。这次对话的几个要点：LLM 没有内在动机，也无法自主设定目标；以"用户粘性"为优化目标训练出的模型，并不能产出最有价值的结果（Chen 提到自己花了 20 轮打磨一封毫无意义的邮件，直到 Claude 告诉他直接发出去就行）；专门针对写作指标训练的模型容易"作弊"，比如在每句话里都塞一个比喻（[链接](https://x.com/danshipper/status/2069805581263847467)）。
