### Swyx, DX/Cognition/Latent Space

Swyx floated a contrarian take that Git itself may need to die next — after the PR and the code review go. He estimates 20–40% of code spend is just managing merge conflicts, and points out we don't do line-by-line merges when collaborating with human colleagues; we chat, suggest edits, and let an owner ship. His bet: the future codebase looks more like a Notion or Linear database than .git objects — less efficient but more scalable, the Salty Lesson at work ([link](https://x.com/swyx/status/2065559864559145420)). In a separate thread on "loopcraft," he argued the entire game of the next century is stacking loops effectively — early on you'll need to know when to go *down* a loop when things break, but the bigger leverage is knowing when to go *up* a loop as models improve ([link](https://x.com/swyx/status/2065307558198567206)).

Swyx 抛出一个反共识观点：在 PR 和 code review 之后，Git 本身可能是下一个该死的东西。他估计 20–40% 的研发开销其实只是在处理 merge conflict——而我们跟人类同事协作时根本不会逐行 merge，大家是聊一聊、提点修改建议、由一个 owner 拍板上线。他的预测是：未来的 codebase 会更像 Notion 或 Linear 的 database，而不是 .git objects，效率更低但更 scalable，这就是 Salty Lesson 的体现（[链接](https://x.com/swyx/status/2065559864559145420)）。另一条关于"loopcraft"的帖子里他说，下一个世纪的全部游戏其实就是把 loop 堆得多漂亮——早期你得知道事情出错时怎么"往下"再绕一层 loop 拿可靠性，但随着模型变强，更有杠杆的是知道什么时候"往上"跳一层 loop 拿放大效应（[链接](https://x.com/swyx/status/2065307558198567206)）。

### Amjad Masad, Replit CEO

Replit CEO Amjad Masad shared why Replit refused to build a "tokenmaxxing" leaderboard when enterprise customers asked — they sell outcomes, not tokens for the sake of tokens, and they knew the craze wouldn't last ([link](https://x.com/amasad/status/2065597793998422308)). In a more substantive post, he described how he barely does traditional prompting anymore — his prompts are barely a sentence expressing an outcome, and an orchestrator dispatches parallel agents, a computer-use verifier gives feedback, and security, production, and SEO agents generate fix prompts. His claim: the industry is typically 3–6 months behind what Replit is doing internally ([link](https://x.com/amasad/status/2065452585964949831)). He also reminded builders that if you make money on Replit, you get free credits ([link](https://x.com/amasad/status/2065503810592833560)).

Replit CEO Amjad Masad 解释了当年 enterprise 客户要求 Replit 做"tokenmaxxing" leaderboard 时他们为什么拒绝——Replit 卖的是 outcome，不是为 token 而 token，而且他们当时就知道这股风潮不会持久（[链接](https://x.com/amasad/status/2065597793998422308)）。在另一条更有料的 post 里，他说自己现在几乎不做传统意义上的 prompting 了——他的 prompt 通常只有一句话，描述想要的 outcome，然后由 orchestrator 调度并行 agents，computer-use verifier 给反馈，security、production、SEO 几个 agent 各自生成修复 prompt。他说一句狠话：整个行业通常比 Replit 内部正在做的事落后 3–6 个月（[链接](https://x.com/amasad/status/2065452585964949831)）。他还顺手提醒 builder：你只要在 Replit 上挣到钱，就能拿到 free credits（[链接](https://x.com/amasad/status/2065503810592833560)）。

### Anthropic

Anthropic announced that the US government has issued an export control directive citing national security, suspending all access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5 by any foreign national, whether inside or outside the United States — including foreign national Anthropic employees. As a result they have to abruptly disable Fable 5 and Mythos 5 for all customers to ensure compliance; access to other Claude models is not affected. Anthropic says it believes this is a misunderstanding and is working to restore access ([link](https://x.com/AnthropicAI/status/2065597531644743999)).
