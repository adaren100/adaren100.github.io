### Nan Yu, Linear head of product

Linear's head of product Nan Yu shared a hard-won lesson from building their project-updates agent: their naive one-shot version produced slop because users disengaged their brain. Switching to a multi-turn interactive mode where the agent prompts you for emphasis, importance, and missing context produced much better updates. He frames it as a clean example of introducing friction for a net benefit ([link](https://x.com/thenanyu/status/2067703108344369306)). In a separate post, he defined taste as "making the right choice from a range of plausible options along many dimensions with limited information" — like a chess or go player intuiting a move without calculating ([link](https://x.com/thenanyu/status/2067701849491206399)).

Linear 产品负责人 Nan Yu 分享了他们做项目进展 agent 时踩过的坑：早期 one-shot 版本产出的全是 slop，因为用户直接把脑子关掉了。改成多轮交互模式之后，agent 主动问你"重点放哪里、什么最重要、还缺什么 context"，进展质量立刻上来。他把这称作"引入摩擦反而带来净收益"的典型案例（[链接](https://x.com/thenanyu/status/2067703108344369306)）。另一条 post 里他给"品味"下了个定义：在信息有限、维度众多、备选都说得过去的情况下做出对的选择——就像棋手不算棋谱也能直觉走出好棋（[链接](https://x.com/thenanyu/status/2067701849491206399)）。

### Aaron Levie, Box CEO

Box CEO Aaron Levie published a thoughtful breakdown of what the Applied AI layer looks like at scale, pushing back on the "just a thin LLM wrapper" critique. His playbook has four components: build features that bridge intelligence and workflow (bespoke tools, human-in-the-loop UX, domain-specific data capture); act as a model router balancing frontier models with cheaper ones based on per-task evals; drive implementation and change management via FDEs (data cleanup, process re-engineering, SLAs); and run domain-specific GTM that speaks customers' language. His closing point: even if the bitter lesson eventually solves everything in the limit, enterprises need help changing today, and most of what wins now will still matter no matter how good the models get ([link](https://x.com/levie/status/2067455756795039957)).

Box CEO Aaron Levie 写了一篇有料的长文，分析 Applied AI 这一层在 enterprise 规模化时长什么样，反驳"只是 LLM 上面薄薄一层"的批评。他总结的 playbook 有四块：第一，建好衔接智能和工作流的功能——为 agent 定制工具、设计 human-in-the-loop UX、按业务捕获 context 数据；第二，做 model router，根据每类任务的 eval 在 frontier 模型和便宜模型之间动态调度；第三，靠 FDE 推动实施和变革管理——数据清洗、流程再造、SLA 拉齐；第四，跑 domain-specific GTM，用客户行业的语言去卖。最后他点题：即便 bitter lesson 在极限上能解决一切，enterprise 是"今天"就需要被帮着改变的，现在做对的事情，不论模型变多强都还会重要（[链接](https://x.com/levie/status/2067455756795039957)）。

### Google DeepMind

Google DeepMind published an AI Control Roadmap — a framework for building and managing the advanced AI deployed within Google, premised on the question "what if AI doesn't do what we intend?" rather than assuming alignment ([link](https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2067594863785173257)). Their data shows most issues stem not from bad intent but from agents misinterpreting commands or getting overly enthusiastic about a goal ([link](https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2067594866196877631)). They argue there's a narrow window to embed structural security protocols before multi-agent systems scale globally, and call for AI labs, government, and academia to collaborate on the multilayered approach ([link](https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2067594868180857165)).

Google DeepMind 发布了 AI Control Roadmap——一套用于构建和管理 Google 内部部署的高级 AI 的框架，核心前提不是"假设 AI 会按我们意图行事"，而是"万一它没有呢"（[链接](https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2067594863785173257)）。他们的数据显示，绝大多数问题不是源于恶意，而是 agent 误解了指令、或者太执着于完成一个目标（[链接](https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2067594866196877631)）。他们的判断是：在 multi-agent 系统全球规模化之前，把结构性的安全协议嵌进去的时间窗口很窄，需要 AI 实验室、政府、学界一起把这套多层防护推进起来（[链接](https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2067594868180857165)）。

### OpenAI

OpenAI shared early results from training models to carry beneficial traits into new situations. The most interesting finding was cross-domain transfer: when beneficial-behavior training was limited to health conversations, the model still improved on non-health misalignment, deception, and reward-hacking evals that looked nothing like the training data ([link](https://x.com/OpenAI/status/2067722693714338044)). They also saw the model become harder to steer toward harmful behavior with adversarial prompts while staying responsive to helpful instructions, plus preliminary evidence of greater resistance to harmful fine-tuning ([link](https://x.com/OpenAI/status/2067722695270334549)). They frame it as an early step toward more robustly beneficial and aligned models — as capability grows, reliability, transparency, and helpfulness should grow with it ([link](https://x.com/OpenAI/status/2067722696759329125)).

OpenAI 分享了一组早期结果：训练模型把"对人有益的特质"带到新场景里去。最有意思的发现是 cross-domain transfer——只在 health 对话上做有益行为训练，模型在与训练数据完全不同的非健康场景下的 misalignment、欺骗、reward hacking 评测上也变好了（[链接](https://x.com/OpenAI/status/2067722693714338044)）。他们还看到模型在对抗性 prompt 下更难被引向有害行为，同时仍然对有用指令保持响应，并有初步证据显示它对有害 fine-tuning 的抵抗力增强（[链接](https://x.com/OpenAI/status/2067722695270334549)）。他们把这定位为通向"更稳健地有益且对齐"的模型的第一步——能力提升的同时，可靠性、透明度、对人有用程度也应该一起涨（[链接](https://x.com/OpenAI/status/2067722696759329125)）。

### Andrew Ng, AI educator

AI educator Andrew Ng launched a new course on adding voice to AI agents, built with VocalBridge (an AI Fund portfolio company) and taught by its CEO Ashwyn. The pitch: voice apps historically required choosing between fast voice-to-voice models that sacrifice reliability and accurate speech-to-text pipelines that add latency — this course teaches both. Students build three apps: a voice-interactive game with voice and clicks on a single channel, an agent that gains voice in ~10 lines of code without touching prompts or tools, and an agent that places outbound phone calls via a `make_phone_call` function ([link](https://x.com/AndrewYNg/status/2067653578945359898)).

AI 教育者 Andrew Ng 上线了一门新课程：给 AI agent 加 voice，由 VocalBridge（一家 AI Fund 投的公司）合作，CEO Ashwyn 亲自讲。卖点是：语音应用过去一直要在"快但不可靠的 voice-to-voice 模型"和"准但延迟高的 speech-to-text pipeline"之间二选一，这门课教你两边都要。课上会做三个应用：一个语音和鼠标点击共用一条 channel 的互动游戏；一个不动 prompt 和 tool、只加 ~10 行代码就有 voice 的 agent；以及一个通过 `make_phone_call` 函数主动打外呼电话的 agent（[链接](https://x.com/AndrewYNg/status/2067653578945359898)）。

### Sebastian Raschka, ML researcher

ML researcher Sebastian Raschka caught up on the GLM-5.2 release and calls it the best open-weight model today. Architecturally it builds on GLM-5 / GLM-5.1 and reuses Multi-head Latent Attention (MLA) plus DeepSeek Sparse Attention (DSA) from DeepSeek V3.2. The new piece is IndexShare — a cross-layer reuse trick for DSA where the full sparse-attention top-k indexer runs only once every four layers and subsequent layers reuse those selected token indices, keeping the DSA idea but making 1M-token inference much cheaper ([link](https://x.com/rasbt/status/2067612153020838055)).

ML 研究员 Sebastian Raschka 补看了 GLM-5.2 的发布，他认为这是当前最强的 open-weight 模型。架构上是 GLM-5 / GLM-5.1 的延续，复用了来自 DeepSeek V3.2 的 Multi-head Latent Attention（MLA）和 DeepSeek Sparse Attention（DSA）。新东西是 IndexShare——一个针对 DSA 的跨层复用 trick：完整的稀疏注意力 top-k indexer 每四层只跑一次，后面几层直接复用那批 token indices，DSA 思路不变，但 1M token 推理便宜了一大截（[链接](https://x.com/rasbt/status/2067612153020838055)）。
