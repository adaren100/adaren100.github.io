### OpenAI

OpenAI said agentic tools are reshaping work internally, with employees increasingly using Codex to do longer-running, more complex, and cross-functional work across every department. The company frames its own internal usage as an early signal of how agents may reshape work broadly as they become more capable and widely available ([link](https://x.com/OpenAI/status/2070196105745518913)).

OpenAI 表示，agentic 工具正在重塑公司内部的工作方式，员工越来越多地使用 Codex 来完成更复杂、运行时间更长、跨职能的工作，几乎覆盖公司各个部门。OpenAI 认为自己的内部使用情况是一个早期信号，预示着随着 agent 能力不断提升、应用范围不断扩大，未来工作方式可能发生的变化（[链接](https://x.com/OpenAI/status/2070196105745518913)）。

### Aaron Levie, Box CEO

Box CEO Aaron Levie argued that the US now has de facto AI regulation, predicting frontier-capability models will likely face government review before release. He laid out several implications: the US controls who gets access to frontier intelligence as long as it stays ahead; releases will likely come in less frequent but bigger jumps; other countries will have more incentive to pursue sovereign AI strategies; and open weights become a clear winner since they may remain free of the same controls for now ([link](https://x.com/levie/status/2070310706369712272)). He also detailed how Claude Tag's integration with Box works in practice: rather than giving an agentic coworker access to a user's personal resources, it needs its own scoped access to company tools and data, like sales materials, brand guidelines, product docs, or contracts, so it can act safely as a shared resource across a team ([link](https://x.com/levie/status/2069975251476422664)).

Box CEO Aaron Levie 认为美国现在事实上已经有了 AI 监管，他预测具备前沿能力的模型未来很可能需要经过政府审查才能发布。他列出了几个潜在影响：只要美国在前沿智能上保持领先，就能掌控谁能获得这些能力；模型发布节奏会变慢，但每次跃升的幅度会更大；其他国家会更有动力发展本国主权 AI 战略；而 open weights 模型会是明显的赢家，因为短期内它们可能不受同样的监管约束（[链接](https://x.com/levie/status/2070310706369712272)）。他还详细说明了 Claude Tag 与 Box 集成在实践中的运作方式：与其让 agent 同事直接访问用户的个人资源，更应该给它一套独立的、范围受限的公司工具和数据访问权限，比如销售资料、品牌规范、产品文档或合同，这样它才能作为团队共享资源安全地工作（[链接](https://x.com/levie/status/2069975251476422664)）。

### Aditya Agarwal, South Park Commons General Partner

South Park Commons General Partner Aditya Agarwal argued it's stupidly hard to start a pure software company right now, because customers don't want software, they want outcomes, forcing companies into heavy customization. He says anything horizontal is on a ticking clock, and building something good now requires a heretical 2-3 year view of the world ([link](https://x.com/adityaag/status/2070179913647485344)).

South Park Commons 普通合伙人 Aditya Agarwal 认为，现在做一家纯软件公司难度极高，因为客户想要的不是软件本身，而是结果，这逼着公司不得不大量做定制化工作。他说任何横向产品都像在倒计时，想做出真正好的东西，现在需要一种近乎"异端"的 2-3 年远见（[链接](https://x.com/adityaag/status/2070179913647485344)）。
