### Roblox Product lead Peter Yang

Roblox Product lead Peter Yang resurfaced his number-one lesson from Ryan Carson on shipping 10 PRs a day: the old "do the bare minimum to get the MVP out, don't spend time on systems" advice is now literally reversed. You have to spend a lot of time setting up your documentation and wiring it into a cron job with a skill file — and suddenly you're doing the work of 10 people. The reframe: what used to feel like procrastination (building systems instead of the MVP) is now a prerequisite to shipping effectively with AI agents ([link](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2059029752858775581)).

Roblox 的产品负责人 Peter Yang 又拎出了他从 Ryan Carson 那里学到的第一条经验——关于一天 ship 10 个 PR 的方法论：过去那套"先把 MVP 做出来，别在系统上浪费时间"的建议现在彻底反过来了。你必须花大量时间把文档搭起来、用 skill file 接到 cron job 里——然后你突然就能做 10 个人的活。这种重新定义很有意思：以前那种"我又在拖延，没在做 MVP"的感觉，现在恰恰是用 AI agent 高效 ship 的前置条件 ([链接](https://x.com/petergyang/status/2059029752858775581))。

### Box CEO Aaron Levie

Box CEO Aaron Levie sided with Goldman Sachs' CEO on the optimistic side of the AI-and-jobs debate, with a clean mental model for why job loss isn't the right framing. Even before AI, decades of automation made everything faster and cheaper — and if you assumed the world was static, you would have predicted no jobs left. What actually happens is that we constantly demand more from everything. Some players use the automation to deliver a richer product or service, that raises expectations across the market, and everyone else has to respond. So we end up with more financial analysis from analysts, more comprehensive legal advice, more tailored financial services, better software in niches we never thought we could automate, healthcare providers offering more tests and deeper advice. His takeaway: drop the static-world view and you get a much better picture of how jobs evolve with AI ([link](https://x.com/levie/status/2059025559896883489)).

Box CEO Aaron Levie 站到了高盛 CEO 那一边，对"AI 抢工作"这件事给出了一个很清爽的思维模型，解释了为什么"工作消失"根本就不是正确的框架。即使在 AI 之前，几十年的自动化已经让所有事情都更快、更便宜——如果你假设世界是静态的，你早就该预测出"没工作了"。但真实情况是：人类对所有东西的要求一直在抬升。一旦市场上某些玩家用自动化把产品或服务做得更丰富，整个市场的期待就被拉高，其他人不跟进就没法竞争。所以最终我们看到的是：分析师交付更多财务分析、律师给出更全面的法律建议、金融服务变得更个性化、那些过去没人敢想能自动化的小众领域出现了更好的软件、医疗机构开出更多检查项目和更深入的医嘱。他的结论是：抛掉"世界是静态的"这个假设，你才看得清 AI 会怎么改变工作 ([链接](https://x.com/levie/status/2059025559896883489))。

### FPV Ventures partner Nikunj Kothari

FPV Ventures partner Nikunj Kothari pushed back on the "aren't you a VC, why are you building?" DMs he gets every week. His answer: the space is moving so fast that the only way to stay at the frontier is to build — priors need to be rethought every few months. And it's a lot of fun to poke at the edges of these models and find capabilities that haven't been documented yet. The bitter lesson he sees: automate or get automated ([link](https://x.com/nikunj/status/2058927145519562867)). Separately, he shared one of his favorite Claude Code tricks: reverse-engineering APIs through network sniffing. Many websites are impossible to navigate "deterministically" through the DOM or screenshots, so he points Claude Code at browser_harness (from browser_use) or vanilla Playwright, clicks around the data he wants, and lets Claude Code dig through the network logs to figure out the API structure, auth (usually cookies), and rate limits. He uses it for side projects like his travel CLI and for monitoring sites for intel — and predicts every website will soon need to be headless, with mechanisms for agents to pay sites programmatically, plus a tools.txt analog to llms.txt so agents know what tooling is available ([link](https://x.com/nikunj/status/2058783316753686558)).

FPV Ventures 的合伙人 Nikunj Kothari 回怼了每周都会出现在他 DM 里的那句话——"你不是 VC 吗，怎么还在自己 build？"他的回答是：这个领域变化太快了，唯一能留在前沿的方法就是自己动手——你每隔几个月就要把所有 prior 重新算一遍。而且真的很好玩——亲手去戳这些模型的边界，能发现一些还没人写下来的能力。他看到的 bitter lesson 是：要么你自动化别人，要么你被自动化 ([链接](https://x.com/nikunj/status/2058927145519562867))。另外他分享了一个他最爱的 Claude Code 用法：通过抓网络请求来反向工程 API。很多网站根本没法用 DOM 或截图"确定性地"导航，他的做法是让 Claude Code 接上 browser_use 的 browser_harness（或者直接用 Playwright），自己手动点想要的数据，然后让 Claude Code 去翻 network log，搞清楚 API 的结构、需要什么 auth（大多是 cookie），以及试出 rate limit。他把这套用在自己的 travel CLI、网站监控等各种 side project 上——并预测每个网站很快都需要支持 headless 模式，还得有一套机制让 agent 能给网站付费；llms.txt 之外，我们也很快会需要一个 tools.txt，让 agent 能知道有哪些工具可用 ([链接](https://x.com/nikunj/status/2058783316753686558))。

### OpenClaw founder Peter Steinberger

OpenClaw founder Peter Steinberger continued his dependency purge: he killed Sharp and Jimp and replaced them with photon, a small WebAssembly module that runs compiled Rust for image processing. The result is 2MB versus 140MB, which is the kind of swap that makes the whole shipping story easier ([link](https://x.com/steipete/status/2058922222790525272)). His other PSA is for skill authors: ask your agent to be token-efficient and relax the grammar — too many skills "write books" in their description, and all that gets loaded into every context. He even wrote a skill that finds the worst offenders ([link](https://x.com/steipete/status/2058917897590673525)).

OpenClaw 创始人 Peter Steinberger 继续给自己的项目做依赖瘦身：他干掉了 Sharp 和 Jimp，用 photon 替换——一个跑编译后 Rust 的小型 WebAssembly 图像处理模块。结果是 2MB 对 140MB，这种替换会让整个 ship 流程都轻松很多 ([链接](https://x.com/steipete/status/2058922222790525272))。他对 skill 作者的另一个提醒是：让你的 agent 在写 skill 时讲究 token 效率，语法可以放松一点——太多 skill 在 description 里"写小说"，而这些内容会被一股脑塞进每一个 context。他甚至专门写了一个 skill，用来揪出那些写得最啰嗦的"罪魁祸首"([链接](https://x.com/steipete/status/2058917897590673525))。
